# 七杀 Seven Killings (Qi Sha) — The Warrior and the Rival

## Core Concept
Seven Killings (Qi Sha) represents the element that controls the Day Master with the same polarity. It is a powerful, direct, and aggressive energy. In the family, it represents the father (for women) or a rival. In the world, it represents challenge, pressure, enemies, and the force of extreme change.

## Psychological Profile

**Archetype:** The warrior, the pioneer, the rebel, the high-stakes risk-taker.

**Positive Traits:**
- **Courage:** Qi Sha people are not afraid of a fight. They thrive in crisis and are often the first to act when things go wrong.
- **Decisiveness:** They make choices quickly and forcefully. They don't have time for nuance when action is required.
- **Pioneering Spirit:** They are excellent at starting things from scratch or breaking into new territory.
- **Strategic Thinking:** Under the aggression, there is often a very sharp, tactical mind at work.

**Negative Traits (when excessive or unfavorable):**
- **Ruthlessness:** They can be incredibly harsh on themselves and others. "The end justifies the means."
- **Impulsiveness:** They may act out of anger or a need for dominance before thinking things through.
- **Paranoia:** Because they see the world as a battlefield, they may struggle to trust anyone.
- **Volatility:** They are prone to sudden, explosive outbursts of energy or emotion.

## Practical Manifestations

**In Career:**
They excel in high-pressure environments: military, law enforcement, surgery, venture capital, and start-up leadership. They are the "troubleshooters" of the world. However, they may struggle with traditional authority because they want to lead, not follow.

**In Relationship:**
For women, this star traditionally represents the "rival" or the "intense lover" (distinct from the stable "husband" of Zheng Guan). In modern terms, it represents a desire for a partner who is powerful, challenging, and perhaps a bit unpredictable. Relationships are often intense and transformative.

**Wealth Dynamics:**
Qi Sha can be a source of massive wealth, but it's "warrior wealth" — gained through high-stakes gambles, competition, or intense effort. It is not stable wealth. A person with a strong Qi Sha star needs to be very careful not to lose everything in a single, impulsive move.

## Symbolic Meaning
- **The Crucible:** The pressure that turns coal into diamond.
- **Extreme Change:** The force that breaks old structures to make way for the new.
- **The Shadow Warrior:** The part of us that needs to conquer to feel alive.

## The Qi Sha Question:
"What are you fighting? And is the fight making you stronger, or just making you harder?"
