# Wood (木)

## Header
**Yang form:** 甲 jiǎ (the tall tree, the oak, the cedar). **Yin form:** 乙 yǐ (the grass, the vine, the flowering plant). **Season:** spring. **Direction:** east. **Body:** liver and gallbladder, tendons, eyes. **Generates:** fire. **Controls:** earth. **Controlled by:** metal. **Drained by:** fire. **Resented by:** earth (when wood is weak earth piles up against it; when wood is strong it overruns earth).

## Core Meaning
The element of beginning, of upward growth. Wood is the rising sap of spring, the seed that has cracked and is pushing toward light. Read as the principle of birth, of ambition, of the green push that wants to grow.

## Yang Wood (甲)
Tradition reads 甲 as the great tree — the trunk that stands through storms, the figure who builds upward and casts long shadow. A 甲 day master is often a person of straight bearing, ambition, and slow stubborn growth. Tall, principled, sometimes inflexible. The wood that splits cleanly under the axe of metal but does not bend easily.

## Yin Wood (乙)
Tradition reads 乙 as the grass and the climbing vine — wood that has learned softness. A 乙 day master is often adaptive, charming, sometimes manipulative; the figure who survives by finding the support to climb on. The 乙 person is harder to break than the 甲 person but more easily talked into things.

## When Strong
Strong wood (born in spring, surrounded by water and wood, or by friendly month) wants something to do. The strong wood person is the founder, the builder of institutions, the one with vision and stamina. Strong wood needs metal to shape it — a strong 甲 with strong 庚 in the chart is the timber that becomes the house. Without metal, strong wood overgrows itself.

## When Weak
Weak wood (born in autumn, surrounded by metal, or facing strong earth) needs support. The weak wood person needs water (resource) to nourish and friends (more wood) to brace. Weak wood facing too much metal is the sapling cut down before it can grow; the figure may have ambition without the strength to carry it.

## Keywords
spring, the rising sap, growth, ambition, straightness (yang), flexibility (yin), the tree, the vine, the green push
