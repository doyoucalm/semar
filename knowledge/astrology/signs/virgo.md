# Virgo (♍)

## Glyph
The maiden with a sheaf of wheat. The glyph shows an M with a closed loop — the body containing itself, the harvest tied.

## Header
**Element:** Earth. **Modality:** Mutable. **Ruler:** Mercury. **Exaltation:** Mercury (traditional). **Detriment:** Jupiter / Neptune (modern). **Fall:** Venus. **Body:** Intestines, digestion, the abdomen. **Mythic figure:** Demeter the harvest goddess; Astraea who lifted from earth at the end of the golden age.

## Core Meaning
The body refined through work. Virgo is the sixth sign, the late-summer harvest, the moment when what was grown must be sorted. Read as discernment, craft, service — the figure whose attention is fine enough to see what others miss.

## Strength
Tradition reads Virgo as the craftsperson, the editor, the healer, the figure whose love is expressed through care of details. The mutable earth is adaptable practice: Virgo placements adjust the technique to the material, refuse a shortcut that compromises the work, and find dignity in tasks that look small from outside. Mercury's earthy rulership gives Virgo a precise rather than dazzling mind — a mind that wants the right word, the right measurement, the right knife.

## Shadow
The same precision turned anxious. Virgo placements may pick at the imperfect until the whole thing collapses, may serve until they have no self left, may use their critical faculty to make themselves feel safe through finding faults. The shadow is the figure who polishes the doorknob while the house burns.

## In a Chart
The house holding Virgo shows where the chart wants to refine, edit, and serve — where the figure does the small thankless work that holds the larger structures up. Mercury's condition colours the quality of that attention: a dignified Mercury makes the Virgo placement an artist of detail; an afflicted Mercury makes it a worrier who cannot stop checking. The Ascendant in Virgo gives the body the carriage of someone always slightly tidying.

## Keywords
craft, the harvest, mutable earth, service, discernment, the editor, anxiety (shadow), perfectionism (shadow), self-erasure through service (shadow)
