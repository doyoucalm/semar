# Pisces (♓)

## Glyph
Two fish bound by a cord, swimming in opposite directions. The shape of the two pulls — toward the depths and toward the surface, toward dissolution and toward return.

## Header
**Element:** Water. **Modality:** Mutable. **Traditional ruler:** Jupiter. **Modern ruler:** Neptune. **Exaltation:** Venus. **Detriment:** Mercury. **Fall:** Mercury. **Body:** Feet, lymphatic system, the immune system. **Mythic figure:** Aphrodite and Eros disguised as fish escaping Typhon; the dissolving sea before the spring.

## Core Meaning
The dissolution before the return. Pisces is the twelfth sign, the end of the zodiac, the last weeks of winter when the boundaries between things soften. Read as compassion, imagination, the figure whose porosity is both gift and difficulty.

## Strength
Tradition reads Pisces as the mystic, the artist, the healer, the figure whose feeling touches the feelings of others without translation. The mutable water is dissolving water: Pisces placements melt the borders that other signs maintain. Jupiter's traditional rulership gives Pisces a generosity that gives without measure; Neptune's modern overlay gives it the access to the imaginal, the dreaming, the unsayable. The sign at its best is the chapel where the whole zodiac comes to be forgiven.

## Shadow
The same porosity turned to loss of self. Pisces placements may absorb the moods of every room, may use intoxication or fantasy to escape the work in front of them, may play victim or martyr because the boundary that would protect them is not built. The shadow is the figure who has dissolved so completely that they can no longer be found.

## In a Chart
The house holding Pisces shows where the chart cannot maintain a hard boundary — where the figure dreams, suffers, serves, and dissolves. Jupiter's condition (and Neptune's) colours how the porosity is held: a dignified Jupiter makes the Pisces placement compassionate without sinking; an afflicted Neptune makes it confused. The Moon in Pisces is an emotional body fluent in the world's grief; the Ascendant in Pisces gives a body that looks slightly elsewhere even when present.

## Keywords
the dissolution, mutable water, compassion, imagination, the mystic, the artist, porosity, escapism (shadow), self-loss (shadow), martyrdom (shadow)
