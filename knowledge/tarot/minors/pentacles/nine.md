# Nine of Pentacles

## Image
A woman in a rich brocade gown stands in a vineyard, a falcon resting hooded on her gloved hand. Nine pentacles are arranged among the vines. Her expression is serene. A snail crawls at her feet.

## Core Meaning
The prosperous garden of one's own. The figure has built a refined material life and now enjoys it — the vineyard producing, the falcon trained, the garden cultivated. Read as solitary abundance, the well-earned independence.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the woman who has succeeded by her own labour and now stands in the harvest she has planted. The hooded falcon is significant — the wilder parts of her nature are kept disciplined; her independence is not feral. The snail is the slow accumulation that made the garden possible. The card praises the long view rewarded.

## Reversed
The garden over-tended, or the figure isolated by her independence. Sometimes the wealth has become a wall against intimacy; sometimes the discipline of the falcon has tipped into self-denial.

## Keywords
solitary abundance, the vineyard, well-earned independence, refinement, the trained falcon, slow accumulation, isolation (reversed), over-discipline (reversed)
