# Knight of Swords

## Image
A knight charges full-tilt on a grey-white horse, sword raised. His armour is detailed; his horse strains forward. Trees behind bend in the wind.

## Core Meaning
The charging intellect. The Knight of Swords rides hard, argues hard, finishes hard. Read as the figure who acts on the idea — the lawyer in motion, the writer at full speed, the activist who will not slow down to be polite.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the rapid intellectual offensive — the lawsuit filed, the manifesto published, the call placed without hesitation. The horse is galloping; the speed is part of the meaning. There is courage here, and the willingness to lead with the sword's edge.

## Reversed
Speed without thought, or argument as bullying. The Knight reversed is the figure who has confused velocity with wisdom — the angry email, the published opinion that needed another draft, the campaign run too fast.

## Keywords
the charging intellect, rapid offensive, argument in motion, the activist, sword raised, recklessness (reversed), bullying (reversed)
