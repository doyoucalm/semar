# Eight of Pentacles

## Image
A craftsman sits at a workbench in front of a tree, chisel and hammer in hand, working on a pentacle. Five pentacles are already mounted on a vertical post; one rests at his feet; he holds the seventh; the eighth is being worked. A village is in the distance.

## Core Meaning
The master at his craft. The figure is fully absorbed in the work — not yet ornate, not yet finished, just steadily making one good thing after another. Read as apprenticeship and mastery in active form, devoted craft, the work that becomes its own meaning.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the long obedience of mastering a skill — the years of practice, the daily showing-up, the satisfaction that comes from being good at the thing one has been doing for a long time. The village in the distance is the world the work serves, but the work itself is in the foreground. The card praises the doer who has chosen depth over distraction.

## Reversed
The work mechanical, or the figure burning out. Sometimes the craft has lost its meaning — the same motion repeated without the heart that made it craft. Sometimes the figure has overworked.

## Keywords
craft, mastery, the long obedience, devoted work, apprenticeship in motion, the chisel, mechanical work (reversed), burnout (reversed)
