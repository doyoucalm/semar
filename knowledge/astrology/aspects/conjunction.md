# Conjunction (☌)

## Header
**Glyph:** ☌. **Angular distance:** 0°. **Default orb:** 8°–10° (tighter for outer-planet conjunctions). **Class:** neutral — takes character from the planets involved.

## Core Meaning
The fusion. The conjunction puts two bodies in the same place — same sign, same degree, same house. Read as a single fused function rather than two functions in dialogue. The conjunction is not good or bad in itself; it takes its character from the planets involved.

## Upright
Tradition reads the conjunction as the union of the two principles. A Sun-Mercury conjunction makes thought and identity inseparable; a Venus-Jupiter conjunction enlarges what is loved; a Mars-Saturn conjunction couples drive with discipline (or with frustration). Modern practice notes that two planets in conjunction often act as a single signature in the life — they cannot easily be experienced apart.

## Variants
A planet within 8° of the Sun is **combust** — overwhelmed by the Sun's light. A planet within 17 minutes of arc (about 0.28°) is **cazimi**, "in the heart of the Sun" — traditionally an exceptionally fortunate placement, where the planet is empowered rather than burnt. Combust planets in horary tradition signal that the planet's themes are unable to act freely; cazimi planets are read with special weight.

## In a Chart
The sign and house of the conjunction colours the fused function strongly. Aspects from other planets to the conjunction land on both bodies at once. The tighter the orb, the more fused the experience; conjunctions within 1°–2° often feel like a single planet operating with two natures.

## Keywords
fusion, union, the same place, the single signature, combust, cazimi, the coupled function
