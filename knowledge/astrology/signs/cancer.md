# Cancer (♋)

## Glyph
The crab — though the glyph is often read as two yin curls, breasts, or the embracing claws. The shape of holding, the shape of return.

## Header
**Element:** Water. **Modality:** Cardinal. **Ruler:** Moon. **Exaltation:** Jupiter. **Detriment:** Saturn. **Fall:** Mars. **Body:** Chest, breasts, stomach. **Mythic figure:** the crab sent by Hera against Heracles; the lunar mother.

## Core Meaning
The container of feeling. Cancer is the fourth sign, the summer solstice, the moment when the year tilts toward its inner life. Read as belonging, memory, the figure whose work is to make a place where others can be held.

## Strength
Tradition reads Cancer as the mother, the keeper of the home, the figure whose feeling has long roots. The cardinal water is initiating emotion: Cancer placements begin relationships, found families, build the kitchens where the rest of the chart will eat. The Moon's rulership gives Cancer the lunar rhythms — the high tide of fullness, the low tide of withdrawal — and the sign honours both phases.

## Shadow
The same water turned protective to the point of suffocation. Cancer placements may cling, may turn their feeling into a fortress, may keep a wound polished for decades because the wound has become identity. The crab's shadow is the figure who has retreated into the shell and forgotten the way out.

## In a Chart
The house holding Cancer shows where the chart needs belonging — where the figure builds nests, where memory accumulates, where the work is private rather than public. The Moon's condition is the key: the Moon's sign, house, and phase tell how the Cancer house is actually inhabited. The Sun in Cancer is the identity rooted in feeling and lineage; the Moon at home in Cancer is the emotional body fluent in tides.

## Keywords
belonging, the home, cardinal water, memory, lineage, the mother, the shell, clinging (shadow), suffocation (shadow)
