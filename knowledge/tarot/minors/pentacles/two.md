# Two of Pentacles

## Image
A young man dances while juggling two pentacles connected by a green ribbon shaped like a lemniscate (∞). Behind him, ships ride the waves of a stormy sea.

## Core Meaning
Juggling. The figure balances two commitments — the second job and the family, the project and the relationship, the immediate and the long-term — by keeping both moving. Read as adaptability, the agile life, the small dance of competing demands.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the period when one is managing multiple things successfully if not gracefully. The lemniscate signals that the balance is dynamic, not static — drop one pentacle and they both fall. The stormy sea suggests the times are not easy but the juggler is up to it.

## Reversed
The juggle dropped, or the figure overloaded. The reversal indicates that one of the commitments is being neglected or that the demands have exceeded the figure's capacity to balance them.

## Keywords
juggling, adaptability, the lemniscate of balance, dynamic management, competing demands, overload (reversed)
