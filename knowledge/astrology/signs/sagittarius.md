# Sagittarius (♐)

## Glyph
The centaur's arrow. A diagonal line crossing itself — the bow drawn, the shaft pointed up and out toward the far horizon.

## Header
**Element:** Fire. **Modality:** Mutable. **Ruler:** Jupiter. **Detriment:** Mercury. **Body:** Hips, thighs, liver, sciatic nerve. **Mythic figure:** Chiron the wounded centaur, the wise teacher; the archer aiming at meaning.

## Core Meaning
The arrow toward meaning. Sagittarius is the ninth sign, the season of the long view, the moment when the year prepares to die and asks what it was for. Read as travel, philosophy, the figure whose hunger is for the horizon and the bigger story.

## Strength
Tradition reads Sagittarius as the philosopher, the teacher, the traveller, the figure who comes back from the foreign country with a story that lasts a generation. The mutable fire is wandering flame — not Aries's strike or Leo's steady burn, but the torch carried into unknown country. Jupiter's rulership gives Sagittarius optimism, generosity, and a love of frameworks large enough to hold many particulars. The sign believes the universe is fundamentally meaningful and worth pursuing.

## Shadow
The same fire turned preachy. Sagittarius placements may mistake conviction for truth, may travel without arriving anywhere internally, may use the largest framework to avoid the hardest particulars. The shadow is the figure whose love of the big picture has made them indifferent to the actual people in front of them.

## In a Chart
The house holding Sagittarius shows where the chart wants more — more meaning, more travel, more study, more distance. Jupiter's condition colours how that pursuit goes: a dignified Jupiter makes the Sagittarius placement a true teacher; an afflicted Jupiter makes it a windbag. The Sun in Sagittarius is an identity organised around the search for meaning; the Moon in Sagittarius needs freedom to feel held.

## Keywords
the arrow, mutable fire, meaning, travel, philosophy, the teacher, the foreign country, preaching (shadow), grandiosity (shadow)
