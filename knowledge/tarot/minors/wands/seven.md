# Seven of Wands

## Image
A figure on higher ground stands with a wand braced before him, fighting off six other wands rising from below. He has the better position; he wears mismatched footwear, suggesting he was caught mid-task.

## Core Meaning
Defence from a hard-won position. The questioner is being challenged for something they have already earned. Read as standing one's ground, the cost of holding the position, conviction tested.

## Upright
The figure is outnumbered but uphill. Tradition reads the card as the defender of one's territory, the person whose ideas are now being attacked because they have started to matter, the leader who must hold the line against a wave of criticism. There is exhaustion implied — but also the dignity of someone whose ground is worth defending.

## Reversed
The defence has become exhausting paranoia, or the figure has given up the high ground out of fatigue. Sometimes the position being defended is no longer worth what is being spent on it.

## Keywords
defence, holding the line, the high ground, conviction tested, hard-won position, exhaustion, paranoia (reversed), abandoned position (reversed)
