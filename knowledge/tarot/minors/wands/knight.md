# Knight of Wands

## Image
An armoured knight on a rearing chestnut horse, charging forward, salamanders embroidered on his cloak. He raises a budding wand. The horse's mane streams; the desert behind has pyramids.

## Core Meaning
Headlong fire. The Knight of Wands is the figure who acts on impulse, rides hard, finishes what he starts (if he can stay focused long enough), and inspires others by the sheer voltage of his motion. Read as drive, charisma, sometimes recklessness.

## Upright
Tradition reads the card as the entrepreneur in motion, the lover whose pursuit is romantic and forceful, the young leader who carries the team forward by example. The horse is rearing rather than walking — there is more energy than control. The salamanders are fire creatures; the knight is wearing his element.

## Reversed
Fire without direction. The Knight reversed is the figure who rushes into projects and abandons them, the lover who pursues hard and leaves, the leader whose impulsivity creates damage. Sometimes the energy has burned itself out before the destination.

## Keywords
the charging knight, drive, charisma, headlong action, pursuit, restlessness, recklessness (reversed), abandoned ventures (reversed)
