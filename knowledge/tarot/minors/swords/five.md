# Five of Swords

## Image
A smirking figure picks up swords from the ground; two other figures walk away, defeated, into a grey landscape. The sky is jagged with storm-cloud.

## Core Meaning
The pyrrhic victory. The card depicts the cost of winning a conflict that was not worth winning, or the gain that has alienated the others. Read as conflict with damage, the win that is also a loss, low-grade dishonour.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the argument won at the cost of the relationship, the conflict where everyone is wounded even though only one figure claims the swords. The grin is small and tight. There is no glory here; the card asks whether the win is worth what it cost.

## Reversed
The figure walking away — toward repair, toward dignity, toward the next chapter that is not founded on this win. Or the conflict cycle deepening, the grievance held longer than it should be.

## Keywords
pyrrhic victory, low conflict, the cost of winning, alienation, dishonour, defeated walking away, repair (reversed)
