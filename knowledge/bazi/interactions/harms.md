# Harms (六害 / 相穿)

## Header
**Type:** branch harm pair. **Pairs:** 子未 / 丑午 / 寅巳 / 卯辰 / 申亥 / 酉戌. **Effect:** mutual hidden damage; the two branches subtly undermine each other.

## Core Meaning
The pairs that hurt each other quietly. The Six Harms are formed by taking each Six Combination pair and substituting the branch on the opposite side of the clash — the result is a pair whose damage is less visible than a clash but more lingering. Read as resentment, betrayal between friends, the wound that takes a long time to show.

## The Six Pairs

| Pair | Common reading |
| --- | --- |
| 子 ↔ 未 | Water ↔ Earth; family or in-law tension |
| 丑 ↔ 午 | Earth ↔ Fire; partnerships that wear at each other |
| 寅 ↔ 巳 | Wood ↔ Fire; the friend who steals position |
| 卯 ↔ 辰 | Wood ↔ Earth; sibling rivalry |
| 申 ↔ 亥 | Metal ↔ Water; the work relationship that hides resentment |
| 酉 ↔ 戌 | Metal ↔ Earth; the friend who cannot be trusted with secrets |

## In a Chart
Harms are softer than clashes and more insidious than combinations gone bad. They suggest relationships that look fine on the surface and slowly fail. Tradition often reads harms between year and month as trouble with elders; between day and hour as trouble with children or with the close partner; between day and month as the difficulty between marriage and family of origin.

## When the Harm Is Active
Harms intensify when triggered by luck pillars or annual transits — the dormant 子-未 in a chart may flare when a 未 year arrives if other supporting conditions are present. Tradition reads harm-triggered years as years of small but persistent trouble: gossip, paperwork problems, the friend who turns cold without explanation.

## In Practice
Modern practice often gives less weight to harms than to clashes or combinations, treating them as secondary. Classical sources, however, take them seriously, particularly in marriage and partnership analysis. A reading that ignores harms can miss a slow leak in an otherwise sound chart.

## Keywords
the quiet wound, hidden resentment, betrayal of trust, the slow leak, the harm that takes years
