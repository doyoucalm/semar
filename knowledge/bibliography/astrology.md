# Astrology Bibliography

Primary, classical, and canonical modern sources used throughout the astrology knowledge base.

## Classical and Hellenistic Sources

- **Vettius Valens.** *Anthologies* (Anthologiae). 2nd century CE.
  Translated by Mark Riley, available freely online. The most extensive surviving Hellenistic astrological text, with practical chart examples.

- **Ptolemy (Claudius Ptolemaeus).** *Tetrabiblos.* 2nd century CE.
  The canonical Hellenistic theory text. Translated by F. E. Robbins, Loeb Classical Library, 1940.

- **Dorotheus of Sidon.** *Carmen Astrologicum.* 1st century CE.
  Foundational text for traditional natal astrology and electional work. Translated by Benjamin Dykes, *Carmen Astrologicum: The 'Umar al-Tabari Translation*, Cazimi Press, 2017.

- **William Lilly.** *Christian Astrology.* London, 1647.
  The most influential English-language traditional astrology text. Reprinted by Astrology Classics, 2004. Indispensable for horary work.

## Modern Foundational Texts

- **Robert Hand.** *Horoscope Symbols.* Whitford Press, Atglen PA, 1981.
  The single most useful modern reference for what each symbol in the chart means. Hand's later work in *Whole Sign Houses* (ARHAT, 2000) is also essential.

- **Liz Greene.** *Saturn: A New Look at an Old Devil.* Weiser, York Beach, 1976.
  The book that opened psychological astrology to a generation. Followed by *Relating* (Aquarian, 1977), *The Astrology of Fate* (Weiser, 1984), and *The Astrological Neptune and the Quest for Redemption* (Weiser, 1996).

- **Demetra George.** *Astrology and the Authentic Self.* Ibis Press, Lake Worth, 2008.
  Bridge between modern psychological astrology and traditional technique. Her *Ancient Astrology in Theory and Practice* (Rubedo, 2019, 2 vols.) is the major modern textbook of Hellenistic technique.

- **Steven Forrest.** *The Inner Sky.* Seven Paws Press, Borrego Springs, 1988.
  Accessible introduction to evolutionary astrology. Forrest's later works extend the system.

- **Howard Sasportas.** *The Twelve Houses.* Aquarian Press, Wellingborough, 1985.
  The canonical modern treatment of the houses. Sasportas' *The Gods of Change* (Penguin, 1989) treats the outer planet transits.

## Aspect Theory

- **Sue Tompkins.** *Aspects in Astrology: A Comprehensive Guide to Interpretation.* Element, Shaftesbury, 1989.
  The standard modern reference on aspects. Tompkins' *The Contemporary Astrologer's Handbook* (Flare, 2006) expands on house and sign interpretation.

- **Bil Tierney.** *Dynamics of Aspect Analysis.* CRCS, Reno, 1980.
  Detailed treatment of T-squares, grand trines, yods, and other configurations.

- **Robert Hand.** *Planets in Transit.* Whitford Press, 1976.
  Reference for transit interpretation.

## Outer Planets, Generational Astrology

- **Richard Tarnas.** *Cosmos and Psyche: Intimations of a New World View.* Viking, New York, 2006.
  Massive treatment of outer-planet cycles and history. Tarnas' *Prometheus the Awakener* (Spring, 1995) treats Uranus specifically.

- **Erin Sullivan.** *Saturn in Transit.* Weiser, Boston, 2000.
  And *Venus and Jupiter: Bridging the Ideal and the Real* (CPA Press, 1996).

- **Liz Greene & Howard Sasportas.** *The Outer Planets and Their Cycles.* CRCS, Reno, 1989.

## House Systems

- **Deborah Houlding.** *The Houses: Temples of the Sky.* Wessex Astrologer, Bournemouth, 1998.
  Historical and traditional treatment of the houses.

- **Robert Hand.** *Whole Sign Houses, the Oldest House System.* ARHAT, Reston, 2000.
  Brief and authoritative on the case for Whole Sign houses.

## Psychological Astrology and Depth Tradition

- **Liz Greene & Stephen Arroyo.** Various works on the psychological school. Arroyo's *Astrology, Karma & Transformation* (CRCS, 1978) is influential.

- **James Hillman.** *The Soul's Code.* Random House, New York, 1996.
  Not strictly astrology but central to the imaginal turn that informed late twentieth-century psychological astrology.

- **Jeffrey Wolf Green.** *Pluto: The Evolutionary Journey of the Soul, Volume I.* Llewellyn, St. Paul, 1985.
  Foundational text for Evolutionary Astrology.

## Indian Astrology (for comparison)

- **B. V. Raman.** *Three Hundred Important Combinations.* IBH Prakashana, Bangalore, 1947.
  Standard reference for Jyotish yogas.

- **David Frawley.** *Astrology of the Seers: A Guide to Vedic/Hindu Astrology.* Lotus Press, Twin Lakes, 1990.
  Accessible introduction to Indian astrology for Western-trained readers.

## Software and Ephemerides

- **Swiss Ephemeris** (Astrodienst). The de facto standard ephemeris used by serious software.
- **Astronomy Engine** (Don Cross). The library used by `packages/astrology` in this project for planet and luminary positions.
