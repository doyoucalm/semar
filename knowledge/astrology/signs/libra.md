# Libra (♎)

## Glyph
The scales. A horizontal bar with a smaller bar resting on it — balance imaged.

## Header
**Element:** Air. **Modality:** Cardinal. **Ruler:** Venus. **Exaltation:** Saturn. **Detriment:** Mars. **Fall:** Sun. **Body:** Kidneys, lower back, skin. **Mythic figure:** Themis, the goddess of divine order; the scales weighing the heart against the feather of Ma'at.

## Core Meaning
The encounter with the other. Libra is the seventh sign, the autumn equinox, the moment when day and night meet again as equals. Read as relationship, fairness, the work of holding two perspectives at once.

## Strength
Tradition reads Libra as the diplomat, the artist, the partner, the figure who builds beauty between people. The cardinal air is initiating connection: Libra placements begin friendships and partnerships and aesthetic projects. Venus's airy rulership gives Libra a love of harmony as a constructed thing — the well-set table, the negotiated peace, the room where the colours agree. The sign's gift is the capacity to consider the other party's case fully.

## Shadow
The same balance turned indecisive. Libra placements may weigh until the moment for action has passed, may seek harmony at the cost of honesty, may merge so fully with the partner that the self is no longer findable. The shadow is the figure who chooses peace over truth and finds, at the end, that they have neither.

## In a Chart
The house holding Libra shows where the chart needs others — where the figure works through partnership, where decisions cannot be made alone, where beauty matters. Venus's condition colours how the relating happens: a dignified Venus makes the Libra placement gracious; an afflicted Venus makes it conflict-avoidant. Saturn in Libra is exalted, suggesting the long discipline of justice. The Descendant in Libra gives partnership a central role in the life.

## Keywords
relationship, the encounter, cardinal air, balance, the diplomat, fairness, indecision (shadow), peace-at-any-cost (shadow), merging (shadow)
