# The Fool (0)

## Image
A young figure stands at a cliff's edge in bright morning sun. A small dog leaps at the figure's heels — alarmed, devoted, or playful, depending on the eye. A white rose is held lightly in one hand; the other carries a slim staff with a bundle. A snow-capped mountain rises far behind. The Fool wears patterned, festival clothing — beautiful and unsuited to the journey ahead.

## Core Meaning
The first card and the last. The Fool is the soul before the journey, unburdened by what is about to be learned. Every card that follows is a station on the road this one is about to walk. In a reading, the Fool returns the questioner to the beginning of something: a leap, a new chapter, a stance of trust in the absence of evidence.

## Upright
The Fool moves before knowing where the ground is. This is read as openness, faith, and the precise kind of courage that is indistinguishable from naïveté at the moment of stepping. It is the spirit of the apprentice and the spirit of the master who has returned to apprenticeship. The cliff is not a warning; the cliff is the only place a beginning can be made. The dog — instinct, the body, the loyal animal companion — comes along whether invited or not. Tradition reads the white rose as purity of intent.

## Reversed
The same courage turned into recklessness, or the same openness collapsed into refusal to look. Reversed, the Fool may indicate the leap that is actually flight from something behind — a refusal to plan, an evasion. It can also indicate hesitation: the figure has frozen at the cliff's edge, neither stepping forward nor returning to the road. The lesson the upright card offers freely, the reversed card demands by stripping the festival clothes.

## In a Reading
The Fool in the question position points to a beginning whose end cannot yet be seen. In the past position, it marks where the questioner once entered the present situation. In the outcome, it suggests the answer is not "what happens" but "what begins." Beside court cards the Fool indicates youth or a youthful aspect; beside Death the leap requires a real letting-go.

## Keywords
beginning, leap of faith, innocence, trust, the apprentice, journey, unencumbered, fresh start, naïveté (reversed), recklessness (reversed)

## Sources
