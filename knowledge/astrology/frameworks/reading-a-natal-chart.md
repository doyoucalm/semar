# Reading a Natal Chart

## Header
A condensed procedure for reading a natal chart from scratch. Not the only approach. The order below moves from the broadest to the most particular, on the principle that the wider strokes inform the finer detail.

## Step 1 — The Big Three
Begin with the Sun (sign and house), the Moon (sign and house), and the Ascendant (sign and any planet within 5°). These three give the chart's identity, emotional life, and outward presentation. They will not be the whole story, but they are the frame inside which the rest of the chart is read.

## Step 2 — Sect and Hemisphere Distribution
Day chart or night chart? (Sun above the horizon = day; below = night.) This determines which planets are the chart's primary benefic and primary malefic: in day charts, Jupiter is the greater benefic and Saturn the greater malefic; in night charts, Venus is greater benefic and Mars greater malefic. Then look at the hemisphere distribution: heavily eastern (left side) means a self-directed life; heavily western (right side) means a life shaped by others; heavily upper means a public-facing life; heavily lower means a private one.

## Step 3 — Element and Modality Distribution
Count the planets in each element (fire, earth, air, water) and each modality (cardinal, fixed, mutable). A heavy or absent element shapes the figure's temperament. Many planets in fire makes for an enthusiastic chart; few or none, a chart that may need to be lit from outside. Many planets in cardinal makes a starter; many in fixed, a holder; many in mutable, an adapter.

## Step 4 — The Ascendant Ruler
The planet ruling the Ascendant's sign is the chart's captain. Locate it: its sign, house, dignity, and aspects describe the figure's main work and main constraints. A well-placed Ascendant ruler suggests a life that feels coherent; an afflicted one suggests struggle to integrate the various parts.

## Step 5 — Planetary Configurations
Read the major aspect patterns: conjunctions, T-squares, grand trines, oppositions, stelliums. These are the chart's load-bearing structures. The signs and houses involved tell where in the life the structures play out.

## Step 6 — Particular Themes
Now go to the houses that match the figure's question. Career questions: tenth house, its ruler, planets there, Saturn's condition. Relationship questions: seventh house, its ruler, Venus and Moon. Money: second and eighth houses, Venus and Jupiter. The general principles continue to apply: read sign, house, dignity, and aspects in that order.

## Step 7 — Time
Identify the major life-cycle transits: Saturn return (~29, ~58), Uranus opposition (~42), Pluto square (~36–45), Neptune square (~42), Chiron return (~50). These mark the chart's developmental thresholds and frame the question of what season of life the figure is in.

## In a Reading
A reading is a conversation, not a recitation. The procedure above gives the chart's shape; the figure in front of you gives the question. Match the shape to the question — and leave room for what is felt in the room. The voice canon applies: describe rather than prescribe. Read the chart as a season, not a sentence.
