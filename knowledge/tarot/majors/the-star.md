# The Star (XVII)

## Image
A naked young woman kneels by a still pool under a clear night sky. She holds two pitchers; she pours water from one onto the earth and from the other into the pool. A great eight-pointed star shines above her, surrounded by seven smaller stars. A small bird — usually an ibis — rests in a tree behind. Hills and a river complete the scene.

## Core Meaning
Quiet hope after the storm. The Star follows the Tower; it is the card that arrives once the dust has settled and the figure has come down to the water. Read as faith renewed, healing, inspiration in its un-hurried form, and the steady kind of hope that does not promise — only stays. Where the Tower took the clothes off the figures, the Star meets her in that nakedness without shame.

## Upright
The woman's nakedness is not erotic in this card; it is the candour of someone who has nothing left to hide. The two pitchers continue the Temperance imagery — the flow back to the source, the flow out to the world — but the urgency has gone out of the alchemy; this is the cool of evening after the burning of midday. The eight-pointed star is the Star of Ishtar in the older traditions, of Venus in others. Tradition reads the upright card as the long return of inspiration after a hard period, the trust that the source is still flowing, the well found in the dry country.

## Reversed
The hope dimmed. Reversed, the Star may indicate the dispirited stretch where the questioner has lost belief in the future — not despair so much as faith gone flat. On another side, the same reversal points to inspiration that is being spilled rather than poured — talent unused, hope not nourished. The bird in the tree has flown, or has not been heard.

## In a Reading
Beside the Tower, the pair tells the long story of catastrophe and recovery. Beside the Moon, the Star's clarity contrasts the Moon's confusion — what one sees by starlight is steady where moonlight wavers. In a creative reading the Star marks the moment inspiration returns; in a health reading, the recovery that has begun but is not yet complete.

## Keywords
hope, faith renewed, healing, inspiration, the source, candour, Venus / Ishtar, quiet trust, lost faith (reversed), uninspired (reversed)
