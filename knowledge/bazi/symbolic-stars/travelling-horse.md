# Travelling Horse (驿马)

## Header
**Category:** mobility / change. **Anchor:** year or day branch. **Rule:** the year (or day) branch determines a single branch that, if present elsewhere in the chart, brings the Travelling Horse star.

## Lookup
| Year/Day branch | Travelling Horse |
| --- | --- |
| 申, 子, 辰 | 寅 |
| 亥, 卯, 未 | 巳 |
| 寅, 午, 戌 | 申 |
| 巳, 酉, 丑 | 亥 |

The Travelling Horse is always the "long" branch of the triad opposite the one whose peak it answers — the position that breaks the home triad's stillness and points outward.

## Core Meaning
The star of movement. The Travelling Horse represents physical and social mobility — travel, relocation, career changes, the figure whose life cannot stay in one place. Read as restlessness in its productive form, the rider who covers ground.

## In a Chart
A chart with the Travelling Horse in any pillar tends toward mobility. In the year branch, the figure may have moved often in childhood, or come from a family of movers. In the month branch, the career involves travel or change of location. In the day branch, the figure changes residence often or marries someone from elsewhere. In the hour branch, mobility comes through children or in later life.

Multiple Travelling Horse positions can give a figure who cannot settle — the mobility becomes restlessness rather than purposeful movement. Modern practice reads digital travel, immigration, expatriation, and frequent business trips as Travelling Horse expressions.

## When Activated
The star is activated when its branch appears in a luck pillar or annual transit. Travelling Horse years often coincide with relocation, foreign travel, change of company, or the start of a project that requires movement. The activation is intensified when the Travelling Horse branch also clashes with the chart's day branch.

## Keywords
movement, travel, relocation, mobility, the change of place, the road, restlessness (shadow)
