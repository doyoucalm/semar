# The Moon (XVIII)

## Image
A full moon — with a crescent moon's face inscribed in it — hangs in a deep sky. Below, a path runs from the foreground into the distance, between two towers. A wolf and a dog howl at the moon from the path. A crayfish crawls out of a pool in the foreground. Fifteen yods fall from the moon onto the landscape.

## Core Meaning
The realm of dream and shadow. The Moon is the card of what is not clearly seen, of fears that may or may not have a basis in waking life, of intuition unsupported by daylight evidence. Read as the unconscious surfacing, the path through unfamiliar country, the dream that contains the actual problem rather than the dressed-up version of it. The moonlight changes the colours of things; it does not lie, but it does not show plain truth either.

## Upright
The dog and the wolf are the same animal in two states — the tamed and the wild — both responding to the same moon. The crayfish crawling from the pool is the older brain emerging from the deep water of dream. The path goes between the towers and into the hills; it leads somewhere, but the destination is hidden by night. Tradition reads the upright card as the period when one is in the dream-world and must trust the dream, even though waking certainty is unavailable. There is illusion here, and there is also the truth that wears illusion's face. The card is associated with Pisces in the Golden Dawn system.

## Reversed
The moonlight cleared, for better or worse. Reversed, the Moon may indicate the lifting of fear that was illusory all along — relief. On another side, the same reversal points to the further descent into confusion, the dream that has taken over the day. Distinguishing the two readings requires looking at the surrounding cards.

## In a Reading
Beside the Star, the two night cards together describe the texture of waiting — the Star's quiet hope, the Moon's wandering. Beside the High Priestess, two registers of the unconscious — one still pool, one howling field. In a question about anxiety, the Moon often names the fear as dream-fear; in a question about creative work, it is the rich and dangerous country one must walk in to make anything new.

## Keywords
dream, the unconscious, illusion, intuition, hidden fears, Pisces, the wolf and the dog, ambiguity, clearing (reversed), deeper confusion (reversed)
