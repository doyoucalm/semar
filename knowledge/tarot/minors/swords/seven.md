# Seven of Swords

## Image
A figure tiptoes away from a colourful encampment, carrying five swords. Two swords are left planted in the ground behind. He looks back over his shoulder, smirking.

## Core Meaning
Stealth, calculation, getting away with something. The figure is not heroic but clever — and the cleverness has costs. Read as strategy, the unfair shortcut, the small theft, also self-deception.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the cunning move, the secret kept, the work done outside the official channels. Sometimes this is morally neutral — the diplomat's quiet deal, the writer who works in the small hours. Sometimes it shades into dishonesty: the colleague who takes credit, the friend who has more reservations than they are saying.

## Reversed
The stolen thing returned, or the lie compounded. The reversal can mark the moment of confession or the deepening of the deception.

## Keywords
stealth, calculation, cunning, the unfair shortcut, the secret, self-deception, confession (reversed)
