# Queen of Swords

## Image
A crowned woman sits on a throne carved with cherubs and butterflies. She holds an upright sword in her right hand; her left hand is extended in a gesture of beckoning or judgement. A bird flies above. A grey sky and grey clouds; a thin tree line behind.

## Core Meaning
Mind mastered with discernment. The Queen of Swords has lived through grief — sometimes literal widowhood in older readings — and her clarity is not cold but earned. Read as the discriminating mind, the honest counsel, the friend whose feedback is exact.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the woman whose intellectual gifts have been refined by what she has been through. She can name what is going on without performance or sentimentality. The grey sky is significant; her clarity is not bought at the price of pretending to be sunny. She is fair, sharp, and trustworthy with truth.

## Reversed
Clarity gone bitter, or judgement gone cold. The Queen reversed may indicate the figure whose grief has hardened into cynicism, the friend whose feedback has become cruelty, the woman whose mind cuts where it should hold.

## Keywords
discernment, the discriminating mind, honest counsel, the widow's wisdom, the upright sword, bitterness (reversed), cruelty (reversed)
