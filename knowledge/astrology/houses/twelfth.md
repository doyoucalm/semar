# The Twelfth House

## Header
**Cadent.** **Natural sign:** Pisces. **Natural ruler:** Jupiter (traditional), Neptune (modern). **Domain:** the hidden, the unconscious, retreat, confinement, dissolution, undoing, the long surrender.

## Core Meaning
The room before the threshold. The twelfth house is the last house before the Ascendant — the place the figure leaves behind to be born, and returns to at the close of every day. Read as what is hidden, what is dissolved, what the figure cannot see in themselves until something forces it into the light.

## Upright
Tradition reads the twelfth as the house of sorrow, of imprisonment (literal or metaphorical), of large institutions (hospitals, monasteries, prisons), and of the spiritual life. Hellenistic sources call it "Bad Spirit" (kakos daimōn) — the place where the figure's adversities and the unconscious work together. Planets here often operate from behind the curtain: the twelfth-house Sun has an identity partly hidden even from itself; the twelfth-house Venus loves secretly or works in caring institutions; the twelfth-house Saturn carries old griefs.

## In a Chart
The twelfth house ruler's condition shows the quality of the figure's relationship with what is hidden and with what undoes them. The sixth-twelfth axis is the daily-work / surrender axis: the body's daily devotion (sixth) and the body's eventual dissolving (twelfth). Modern practice reads the twelfth as the house of meditation, of psychotherapy, of the artist's solitude — the rooms where the self meets what is larger than the self.

## Keywords
the hidden, the unconscious, dissolution, retreat, the long surrender, sorrow, the spiritual life, the institution, what is behind the curtain
