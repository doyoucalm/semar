# Four of Cups

## Image
A figure sits under a tree, arms crossed, looking down at three cups before him. A fourth cup is offered from a cloud-hand — he does not see it. His expression is bored or contemplative.

## Core Meaning
The unseen offering. The figure has what he had; what is being offered next is in his blind spot. Read as apathy, contemplation, the emotional flatness that arrives when one has stopped looking up.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the period of emotional retreat, the figure who has had enough and is not yet ready to receive more, or the depression that does not see the cup the world is holding out. Sometimes meditation; sometimes the dull patience before a turn.

## Reversed
The figure looks up, or the figure sinks further. The reversal can mark renewed interest in the world after withdrawal, or the deeper retreat that has become detachment.

## Keywords
apathy, withdrawn, the unseen cup, emotional flatness, contemplation, renewed interest (reversed), deeper retreat (reversed)
