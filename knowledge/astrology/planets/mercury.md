# Mercury (☿)

## Glyph
A crescent above a circle above a cross — the lunar mind on the solar centre on the cross of matter. The messenger's caduceus implicit.

## Header
**Domicile:** Gemini, Virgo. **Exaltation:** Virgo (15°). **Detriment:** Sagittarius, Pisces. **Fall:** Pisces. **Sect:** ambivalent (takes its sect from proximity to Sun). **Mythic figure:** Hermes; Mercury the messenger; Thoth; the trickster.

## Core Meaning
The mind in motion. Mercury is thinking, speaking, learning, the mechanism by which the figure translates the world into language and the language back into the world. Read as the function that connects — between inside and outside, between this idea and that, between speaker and listener.

## Upright
Tradition reads Mercury as the scribe, the merchant, the messenger, the youth. The sign holding Mercury describes the texture of the mind — fast or slow, abstract or concrete, sensual or critical. The house holding Mercury describes where most of the mental work happens. A dignified Mercury makes a mind clear, articulate, capable of distinguishing what matters from what is only noise.

## Afflicted
The same mind tangled. A Mercury in detriment, fall, combust the Sun, or in hard aspect to malefics may indicate a figure whose thinking is too fast, too slow, too anxious, or too disconnected from the body. Tradition watches Mercury retrograde with particular care: signed contracts, fragile communication, miscarried messages. Pre-modern astrologers found native retrograde Mercury people to be unusually reflective rather than unusually broken.

## In a Chart
Mercury's sign is the answer to "how does this person think?" — Mercury in Capricorn structures; Mercury in Aquarius systems; Mercury in Pisces dreams. Mercury's aspects show what is connected to the mind — Mercury-Mars sharpens to a point; Mercury-Venus softens to charm; Mercury-Saturn slows and deepens. Mercury never travels more than 28° from the Sun, so its sign is either the Sun's or one of the two adjacent signs.

## Keywords
the mind, the messenger, language, learning, exchange, the merchant, anxiety (shadow), trickery (shadow)
