# Suit of Pentacles — Earth

## Element & Sphere
**Earth.** The suit of body, money, work, craft, and the material world taken as ground. Where Wands burn and Cups flow and Swords cut, Pentacles weigh — they ask what is real, what holds, what feeds the body and pays the rent. In life-domain terms: livelihood, possessions, health (in its physical aspect), the long apprenticeship of a craft, the slow patience of growing roots in a place.

## Astrological Association
The earth signs — Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn. Taurus carries the suit's sensual and abundant qualities; Virgo its precision and craft; Capricorn its long climb and patient ambition.

## Visual Signature
Each pentacle is a coin or disc inscribed with a five-pointed star. In the Rider-Waite-Smith deck the scenes are often workshops, gardens, hearths, marketplaces — the places where matter is being shaped and exchanged. Figures are often shown in mid-task, not at rest.

## Pip Progression
The Ace is the gift of earth, a coin offered from a cloud-hand into a flowering garden. The two through ten move through the material life — juggling (2), the apprentice at work (3), the miser holding too tight (4), the cold winter outside the lit church (5), giving with measure (6), patient cultivation while the harvest is uncertain (7), the master at his craft (8), the prosperous garden of one's own (9), and the family wealth handed down through generations (10).

## Court Archetypes
- **Page of Pentacles** — the apprentice, the careful young student, news of money or of a new project taking shape; the figure who studies a coin attentively.
- **Knight of Pentacles** — the steady worker, the figure who rides slowly and arrives where he said he would; sometimes overly cautious.
- **Queen of Pentacles** — earth mastered with abundance; she keeps the garden and the books with the same care, generous host of the material life.
- **King of Pentacles** — earth mastered with sovereignty; the builder, the patron, the figure whose wealth is matched by his stewardship.

## Reading Note
Pentacles answer questions of money, health, work, home, and the literal "what shall I do tomorrow." In health they often point to the body's structural aspects; in work, to craft and to compensation; in relationship, to whether the union is grounded in shared material life or floats above it.
