# Five of Pentacles

## Image
Two beggars — one on crutches, one in rags — walk through snow outside a brightly lit church window. The window depicts five pentacles in a tree pattern. The figures do not look up at the window.

## Core Meaning
Hardship outside the warm building. The card depicts material want, illness, exclusion — and the strange detail that help is visible (the lit church behind them) but the figures do not see it. Read as poverty, alienation, the cold time.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as financial hardship, illness, the loss of position or community. The unseen church is the central note: aid is closer than the figures know — institutional help, spiritual community, friends who would be willing if asked. The card invites the questioner to consider who they have stopped reaching to.

## Reversed
The figures turn toward the church door, or the cold deepens. The reversal can mark the return to community after long isolation, or the further withdrawal into pride that refuses help.

## Keywords
hardship, exclusion, the cold time, financial want, unseen help, illness, return to community (reversed), pride (reversed)
