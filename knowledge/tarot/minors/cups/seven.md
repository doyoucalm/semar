# Seven of Cups

## Image
A figure looks up at seven cups floating in a cloud, each containing a different vision — a face, a draped figure, a snake, a castle, jewels, a wreath, a dragon. The figure is silhouetted; we cannot see his choice.

## Core Meaning
The proliferation of possibility. The cups offer different futures, fantasies, ambitions — all visible at once, none yet chosen. Read as the moment of overwhelm with options, daydream, fantasy, the difficulty of choosing among many tempting visions.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the imagination running unchecked, the project list with too many entries, the lover with too many fantasies and too few commitments. The cups are real possibilities and also potential illusions — the wreath of victory floats next to the dragon of danger.

## Reversed
Choice made, or fantasy collapsed into the literal. Sometimes the reversed card marks the painful return to one path; sometimes the further drift into delusion.

## Keywords
fantasy, options, daydream, the cloud of possibilities, illusion, choice deferred, choice made (reversed)

## Sources

- Joan Bunning, *Learning the Tarot* (Weiser, 1998).
