# Four of Wands

## Image
Four wands form a canopy decorated with garlands of fruit and flowers. Two figures with bouquets greet the viewer beneath it. A small village is in the background, with a manor house. The sky is gold.

## Core Meaning
The celebration at the established threshold. Read as homecoming, the milestone reached, the marriage canopy, the harvest festival, the moment a community gathers to mark what has been built.

## Upright
This card sits at the friendly end of the suit. Tradition reads it as the wedding, the housewarming, the project's successful launch party, the family gathering. The four wands form a chuppah-like structure — the marriage canopy of Jewish tradition — but the symbolism is broader: this is any threshold of belonging that has been crossed in good company.

## Reversed
Celebration deferred, or hollow — the party that goes through the motions without the substance. Sometimes the threshold has been crossed without the community to mark it.

## Keywords
celebration, milestone, homecoming, the canopy, harvest, threshold crossed, community, hollow celebration (reversed)
