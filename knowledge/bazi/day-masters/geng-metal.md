# 庚金 Geng Metal — The Axe and the Sword

## Classical Source (滴天髓 Di Tian Sui)

> 庚金带煞，刚健为最。得水而清，得火而锐。土润则生，土干则脆。能赢甲兄，输于乙妹。

**Translation:** Geng Metal carries a killing intent; its firmness is foremost. Meeting Water (Ren) it becomes clear; meeting Fire (Ding) it becomes sharp. If the Earth is moist, Geng lives; if Earth is dry, Geng is brittle. It can conquer its elder brother Jia (Wood), but yields to its younger sister Yi (Wood).

## Classical Source (子平真诠 Zi Ping Zhen Quan)

> 庚为顽金，得火而利。

**Translation:** Geng is raw and unrefined metal — only with Fire does it become sharp and useful.

## Modern Interpretation

**Archetype:** The axe, the sword, the raw ore, the iron beam. Geng Metal is the force of decisive action. It is the clarity of a blade that cuts through confusion. It is the hardest and most "exterior" of the Day Masters.

**Core Nature:**
庚金之人 (Geng Metal people) are the "warriors." They are courageous, direct, and sometimes intimidatingly efficient. They have a massive sense of justice. Like a sword, they are defined by their edge — their ability to make choices, set boundaries, and enforce standards. They do not do things halfway.

**Psychological Profile:**
Geng Metal is motivated by challenge. They need something to conquer, solve, or protect. They find deep satisfaction in being "forged" by life's difficulties. They are the friends who tell you the harsh truth you need to hear, even if it hurts.

They are the "protectors." They are intensely loyal to their "tribe" and will go to war for those they love. They have a natural "command" presence. They are the first to step into a crisis and the last to leave.

**Shadow Side:**
Ruthlessness. They can become so focused on the "result" that they crush people's feelings in the process. They can be incredibly harsh on themselves, believing that only through suffering or "forging" are they valuable. If they lack "fire" (Ding), they are just a heavy, useless lump of iron.

There is also a risk of "brokenness" — Geng Metal that has been clashed too many times without enough "moisture" (Earth/Water) can become brittle and snap. "Shattering" happens when their rigid principles meet a reality they cannot cut through.

**What Geng Metal Needs:**
- Fire (丁 Ding) — To refine. Raw Geng Metal is useless without the precise heat of Ding (the forge). This is the mentor, the discipline, or the clear goal that shapes Geng into a tool.
- Water (壬 Ren) — To clean and sharpen. Ren Water (the ocean) is where the sword is tempered. This is the wisdom and emotional fluidity that keeps Geng from becoming too brittle.
- Wood (甲 Jia) — Geng needs Wood to have a purpose (an axe needs a tree). This is the project or the person Geng protects/manages.
- Earth (戊/己) — Moisture for the "ore" to grow. Dry Earth (Fire-heavy) makes Geng brittle.

**Seasonal Dynamics:**
- 春 Spring (Yin/Mao month): In their "active" season. Busy cutting and managing. Needs Fire to stay refined.
- 夏 Summer (Si/Wu month): Danger of melting. High pressure. Needs Water (Ren) to temper the heat.
- 秋 Autumn (Shen/You month): At their peak strength. This is the harvest. Powerful and potentially dangerous. Needs Fire (Ding) to stay useful.
- 冬 Winter (Hai/Zi month): Cold and damp. Geng becomes very sharp but can be lonely. Needs Fire (Ding) to stay warm and functional.

**In Relationship:**
Geng Metal is the "shield." They provide safety and structure. They are not the most "touchy-feely" partners, but their love is seen in their actions — they provide, they protect, they fix. They need a partner who values their strength and can provide the emotional "tempering" (Water) they lack.

**In Career:**
They excel in law enforcement, surgery, engineering, heavy industry, military, high-level management, and competitive sports. Any role that requires nerves of steel and decisive action.

**The Geng Metal Question:**
When reading for a Geng Metal person, the deep question is: "What are you fighting for? And have you sharpened your blade, or are you just hitting things with a heavy heart?"
