# Taurus (♉)

## Glyph
The bull. A circle with horns rising — the round body of the earth and the curved horns of the moon, fertility's animal.

## Header
**Element:** Earth. **Modality:** Fixed. **Ruler:** Venus. **Exaltation:** Moon. **Detriment:** Mars. **Fall:** Uranus (modern). **Body:** Neck, throat, thyroid. **Mythic figure:** the bull of heaven; Europa's bull; Hathor.

## Core Meaning
The body settled into the world. Taurus is the second sign, the one that takes the spring impulse of Aries and gives it ground — the field ploughed, the seed in the soil, the pleasure of having a body in a place. Read as steadiness, sensuality, the slow accumulation of what is real.

## Strength
Tradition reads Taurus as the gardener, the singer, the figure with the slow hands. The fixed earth is endurance: Taurus placements hold what they hold for decades. The sign loves what can be touched — food, fabric, music, the warm body of a friend. Venus's rulership gives Taurus an aesthetic that prefers the well-made over the new, the seasonal over the trendy. Taurus is the patience of a fruit tree.

## Shadow
The same earth turned heavy. Taurus placements may refuse to move long after the field is exhausted, may confuse comfort with virtue, may hoard against losses that never come. The sign's shadow is the bull that cannot be coaxed out of its pasture, the figure whose love of pleasure has become a refusal of difficulty.

## In a Chart
The house holding Taurus shows where the chart wants to build something solid — where the figure refuses to be rushed, where value accumulates by sitting still. Venus's condition colours the quality of that ground: a dignified Venus makes the Taurus placement generous; an afflicted Venus makes it possessive. The Moon in Taurus is exalted, suggesting an emotional body that finds itself in the comfort of routines and small pleasures.

## Keywords
the body, steadiness, sensuality, fixed earth, patience, the garden, accumulation, stubbornness (shadow), possessiveness (shadow)
