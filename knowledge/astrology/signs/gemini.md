# Gemini (♊)

## Glyph
The twins. Two upright pillars joined top and bottom — the doubled figure, the dialogue, the pair that cannot exist alone.

## Header
**Element:** Air. **Modality:** Mutable. **Ruler:** Mercury. **Detriment:** Jupiter. **Body:** Hands, arms, shoulders, lungs, nervous system. **Mythic figure:** Castor and Pollux, the Dioscuri — one mortal, one immortal.

## Core Meaning
The mind reaching outward. Gemini is the third sign, the moment when the body of Taurus opens its hands to gather — words, news, contacts, fragments of the world. Read as curiosity, communication, the figure who lives in the play of ideas.

## Strength
Tradition reads Gemini as the messenger, the writer, the trader, the figure with the quick tongue and the open notebook. The mutable air is movement of mind: Gemini placements are restless for new inputs and good at translation between unlike things. The sign's myth is the twins — one heel in mortality, one in the immortal — and Gemini placements often hold contradictions without forcing resolution.

## Shadow
The same air turned scattered. Gemini placements may collect everything and finish nothing, may use words to evade rather than reveal, may live so deep in commentary that the body and the heart get little attention. The shadow is the figure who knows about everything and lives in nothing.

## In a Chart
The house holding Gemini shows where the chart wants information — where the figure asks questions, where the work is multiple rather than single, where contacts matter more than commitments. Mercury's condition colours the quality of that mind: a dignified Mercury makes the Gemini placement a clear translator; an afflicted Mercury makes it a fast talker who confuses speed with insight.

## Keywords
the messenger, curiosity, mutable air, words, the twins, dialogue, the quick mind, scatter (shadow), evasion (shadow)
