# Heavenly Net and Earthly Trap (天罗地网)

## Header
**Category:** inauspicious / entanglement. **Heavenly Net:** the pair 戌亥. **Earthly Trap:** the pair 辰巳. **Anchor:** day stem (yang or yin). **Rule:**
- For **yang** day masters (甲丙戊庚壬): the Heavenly Net (戌亥) applies.
- For **yin** day masters (乙丁己辛癸): the Earthly Trap (辰巳) applies.

When both branches of the relevant pair appear in the chart, the star is fully formed.

## Core Meaning
The star of restriction and entanglement. The Heavenly Net catches the upper realms (heaven); the Earthly Trap binds the lower (earth). Together they represent the figure caught — by circumstance, by obligation, by inherited difficulty. Read as the cage that cannot be easily opened, the situation that does not resolve when pushed against.

## In a Chart
A chart with the Heavenly Net or Earthly Trap is read as facing a structural constraint — a family obligation that cannot be set aside, a position from which it is hard to leave, a long-running difficulty that the figure works through over many years. The constraint is not necessarily destructive; some of the chart's deepest work happens within the boundaries the trap imposes.

The star is most intense when both branches of the relevant pair are present in the chart. Partial — only one branch — gives a milder version: a sensitivity to feeling caught, without the full structural binding.

## When Activated
Activation by transit can coincide with periods when the figure feels stuck: a court case, a long illness in a family member, an obligation to remain in a job or place, the slow recovery from a setback. The remedy is rarely escape; the work is to live well within the constraint until it eases.

## Keywords
the net, the trap, entanglement, the structural constraint, the long obligation, the cage, the difficult endurance
