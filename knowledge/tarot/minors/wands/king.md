# King of Wands

## Image
A crowned king sits on a throne carved with salamanders and lions. He holds a budding wand. He wears the suit's red and orange; a green cloak suggests his fire is married to a growing principle. A salamander biting its own tail (the ouroboros of fire) sits at his feet.

## Core Meaning
Fire mastered with vision. Where the Queen carries her own light, the King casts a long line of intent — he leads, he charts course, he inspires others into action. Read as the visionary leader, the founder, the figure whose authority comes from clarity of purpose.

## Upright
Tradition reads the card as the seasoned entrepreneur, the chieftain, the figure whose project draws followers because the vision is real. The salamander ouroboros at his feet is the fire that sustains itself — power that does not need to consume others to keep burning. He is comfortable with command in a way the Knight and the Page have not yet earned.

## Reversed
Vision turned tyrannical, or vision absent. The King reversed may indicate the leader whose authority has hardened into autocracy, the patriarch who rules by force rather than by inspiration. On the other side, the same reversal can show the leader who has lost his fire — the chair occupied, the chart unread, the team adrift.

## Keywords
visionary leader, the founder, command earned, the long view of will, self-sustaining fire, tyranny (reversed), absent leadership (reversed)
