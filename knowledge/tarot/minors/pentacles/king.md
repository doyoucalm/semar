# King of Pentacles

## Image
A crowned king sits on a stone throne carved with bulls' heads. He wears robes embroidered with grapevines. He holds a pentacle in one hand and a sceptre in the other. A castle is in the background; grapevines and fruit grow around him.

## Core Meaning
Earth mastered with sovereignty. The King of Pentacles is the builder, the patron, the figure whose wealth is matched by his stewardship. Read as material sovereignty, the founder of institutions, the senior leader whose decisions create long-term prosperity for others as well as himself.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the seasoned entrepreneur, the landowner who runs his estate well, the patron whose philanthropy is strategic rather than vain. The bulls of the throne are Taurus, the earth's stability. The vines of the robe are growth managed across seasons. He is generous without being foolish, careful without being miserly. The card praises the elder whose competence is comprehensive.

## Reversed
Wealth turned to greed, or stewardship turned to ownership. The King reversed may be the patriarch whose money is now the only language he speaks, the boss whose grip on the business has prevented the next generation from rising, the figure whose competence has hardened into rigidity.

## Keywords
material sovereignty, the builder, the patron, prosperous stewardship, seasoned entrepreneur, greed (reversed), rigidity (reversed)
