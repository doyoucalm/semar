# 癸水 Gui Water — The Rain and the Dew

## Classical Source (滴天髓 Di Tian Sui)

> 癸水至弱，达于天津。得龙而运，功化斯神。不愁火土，不论庚辛。合戊见火，化象斯真。

**Translation:** Gui Water is the ultimate weakness, yet it reaches the celestial ford. Meeting the Dragon (Chen), its transformative power becomes divine. It does not worry about Fire or Earth, and does not depend on Geng or Xin. Combining with Wu and seeing Fire, its transformation is truly genuine.

## Classical Source (子平真诠 Zi Ping Zhen Quan)

> 癸为雨露之水，滋润万物。

**Translation:** Gui is the water of rain and dew — it moistens and nurtures all things.

## Modern Interpretation

**Archetype:** The morning dew, the gentle rain, the mist, the cloud, the ink. Unlike the ocean (Ren), Gui Water is the "soul" of water. It is internal, spiritual, and incredibly pervasive. It is the most "Yin" of all Day Masters.

**Core Nature:**
癸水之人 (Gui Water people) are the "mystics." They are sensitive, intuitive, and possess a deep, internal wisdom. They have an innate talent for "penetrating" through surface appearances. Like a mist, they can be everywhere without being seen. They are the most imaginative and sometimes the most secretive of the Day Masters.

**Psychological Profile:**
Gui Water is motivated by meaning. They need to understand the underlying "truth" of everything. They have a refined "emotional intelligence" — they can feel what others are thinking or feeling before a word is spoken. They are the friends who understand your silence.

They have a "quietly powerful" quality. While others are making noise, Gui Water is silently infiltrating every crack and crevice. They are highly adaptable, able to change their form (like water to mist or ice) to suit the situation. They are the "silent" influencers.

**Shadow Side:**
Insecurity. They can become so focused on their own "depth" that they lose touch with reality. They are the most prone to being "evaporated" (by too much Fire) — losing their sense of self in others' intensity or their own dreams.

There is also a risk of "manipulation" — Gui Water that has no moral ground (Earth) can become like ink that stains everything it touches, or a mist that hides the truth. "Stagnation" happens when their depth becomes a "swamp" of unresolved emotions or secrets.

**What Gui Water Needs:**
- Earth (戊 Wu) — "He Wu Jian Huo" (合戊见火). Gui and Wu "combine." The mountain (Wu) provides the height that allows the cloud (Gui) to form and rain to fall. This is the grounded partner or the practical goal that gives Gui Water a way to be useful.
- Wood (甲/乙) — To express. Water feeds the flower. This is the creative output or the nurturing of others that allows Gui to flow.
- Fire (丙 Bing) — To transform. Sunlight makes the dew sparkle. Bing Fire provides the warmth that allows Gui to be seen and celebrated.
- Metal (庚/辛) — The "source" of pure water. Metal provides the clarity and the "mineral" strength that keeps Gui from becoming "muddy."

**Seasonal Dynamics:**
- 春 Spring (Yin/Mao month): Being exhausted by Wood. Nurturing and busy. Needs Metal (the source) to stay vital.
- 夏 Summer (Si/Wu month): Danger of evaporating. Needs Wood (to hide under) or a "source" (Metal).
- 秋 Autumn (Shen/You month): At their peak. This is when Gui is most clear and brilliant.
- 冬 Winter (Hai/Zi month): Cold and massive. This is when Gui is most independent and powerful. Needs Fire (Bing) to stay fluid.

**In Relationship:**
Gui Water is the "soulmate." They are intensely intuitive and devoted partners. They don't do well with partners who are too "loud" or who don't respect their need for silence and mystery. They need a partner who values their depth and who can provide a stable, grounded presence (Earth).

**In Career:**
They excel in psychology, counseling, spirituality, literature, creative arts, specialized research, and any role that requires a "deep dive" into the human experience or complex systems. They are the healers.

**The Gui Water Question:**
When reading for a Gui Water person, the deep question is: "What are you nourishing? And are you a clear rain that brings life, or a mist that hides the path?"
