# King of Cups

## Image
A crowned king sits on a stone throne that floats on the sea. He holds a cup in one hand, a sceptre in the other. A fish leaps from the water on one side; a ship sails on the other. His expression is composed.

## Core Meaning
Feeling mastered without being suppressed. The King of Cups is the leader who is moved by what he sees but not swept by it — the diplomat, the therapist, the elder whose grief and love have taught him how to be present without disappearing into the other's experience. Read as emotional sovereignty.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the wise mentor, the counsellor whose advice is steady because his own feeling-life is in order, the husband or father whose heart is engaged but not overrun. The throne floating on water is the visual statement: he rules in the element of feeling without being submerged by it.

## Reversed
Feeling repressed into coldness, or feeling acted out through manipulation. The King reversed may be the figure whose composure conceals seething; or the patriarch whose emotional control has become the family's emotional climate.

## Keywords
emotional sovereignty, the wise mentor, composed, the floating throne, diplomatic, repression (reversed), emotional manipulation (reversed)
