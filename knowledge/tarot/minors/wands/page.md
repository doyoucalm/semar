# Page of Wands

## Image
A young figure in fanciful clothes stands holding a budding wand vertically, looking up at the bud at its tip. A desert landscape behind, with three small pyramids on the horizon. The figure's hat is plumed with a long red feather.

## Core Meaning
The young enthusiast. The Page of Wands is the messenger of fire — news of a venture beginning, a creative impulse arriving, a young person whose energy is just starting to find direction. Read as eagerness, the fresh-sparked project, the apprentice of will.

## Upright
This figure is not yet a master of his element; the wand is still budding, the desert is unconquered. Tradition reads the card as the courier of inspiration, the assistant whose enthusiasm carries the larger team, the friend who calls with an idea that turns out to matter. There is unguardedness here, and the cleanness of someone who has not yet learned to be cautious.

## Reversed
Enthusiasm turning to drama, or to false starts. Sometimes the Page reversed is the immature project leader, the figure with more flash than follow-through.

## Keywords
the young enthusiast, fresh-sparked, news of a venture, courier of inspiration, eager apprentice, drama (reversed), false start (reversed)
