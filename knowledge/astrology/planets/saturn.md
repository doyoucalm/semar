# Saturn (♄)

## Glyph
A crescent crossed by a stem ending in a cross — the matter pressing down on the spirit, the lead of the alchemists, the scythe.

## Header
**Domicile:** Capricorn, Aquarius (traditional). **Exaltation:** Libra (21°). **Detriment:** Cancer, Leo. **Fall:** Aries. **Sect:** diurnal (greater malefic by night, less troublesome by day). **Mythic figure:** Kronos; Saturn the elder; Yama the lord of death; the old king at the edge of the kingdom.

## Core Meaning
The principle of limit. Saturn is time, structure, boundary, the part of the figure that has to come down to what is actually possible. Read as the function that says no, that asks for proof, that builds what survives weather.

## Upright
Tradition reads Saturn as the elder, the judge, the teacher of the long discipline, the father in his stern aspect, the bones of the body. The sign holding Saturn describes the kind of work the figure is here to do; the house describes the room where that work is laid down; the aspects describe how the discipline is met. A dignified Saturn does not feel light, but it builds: a career that lasts, a craft that holds up, a self that has paid for itself.

## Afflicted
The same Saturn turned to inhibition. A Saturn in detriment, fall, or hard aspect to lights and personal planets may indicate a figure whose inner critic has eaten the inner singer — the depression, the cold parent, the chronic insufficiency, the body that has carried more weight than was just. Tradition watches the Saturn return (~29 years) as the moment when the figure either accepts the work that is theirs or wastes another cycle.

## In a Chart
Saturn's house shows where the chart is asked to do its hardest, slowest work and where it can become an authority over time. Saturn's aspects describe what is shaped by limit — Saturn to the Moon often gives early loss or emotional restraint; Saturn to Venus often gives serious or delayed love; Saturn to Mars often gives discipline of the will or repeated frustration. The first Saturn return (~age 29) is read as the threshold into adulthood; the second (~58) as the threshold into the elder phase.

## Keywords
limit, time, structure, the elder, discipline, the bones, authority, depression (shadow), inhibition (shadow), the cold father (shadow)
