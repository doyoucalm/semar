# Sextile (✶)

## Header
**Glyph:** ✶. **Angular distance:** 60°. **Default orb:** 4°–6°. **Class:** soft (harmonious, requires more participation than the trine).

## Core Meaning
The opportunity that asks to be picked up. The sextile places two bodies in signs of compatible elements — fire and air (active), or earth and water (receptive). Read as a connection that brings opportunity but only delivers if the figure acts on it.

## Upright
Tradition reads the sextile as a fortunate aspect, less powerful than the trine but more participatory. Where the trine flows on its own, the sextile waits for the figure to engage. A Mercury-Venus sextile gives charm in communication if the figure speaks; a Mars-Jupiter sextile gives expansive courage if the figure acts. Modern practice often notes that sextiles are the most under-used aspects in a chart — they offer real help, but the figure has to reach for it.

## Variants
The Yod (two sextiles meeting at a planet that is quincunx (150°) to the third) is a configuration of three planets where two of them sextile each other and both quincunx a third. The apex planet of a Yod is read as a kind of fated focus — the figure repeatedly returns to its themes through long, indirect routes.

## In a Chart
The houses involved in a sextile show where opportunity becomes available. Sextiles are particularly worth attention when they form between an angle (Ascendant, MC, IC, Descendant) and a personal planet — the figure has an open channel between body and planetary function, but must speak the channel to keep it active.

## Keywords
the opportunity, the soft aspect, the compatible elements, the participation, the Yod, the apex, the under-used resource
