# Tarot Bibliography

Primary and canonical secondary sources used throughout the tarot knowledge base. Cited from the editions held by the project; later printings are equivalent.

## Foundational Texts

- **Arthur Edward Waite.** *The Pictorial Key to the Tarot.* Rider, London, 1910.
  The decisive modern treatment of the Rider-Waite-Smith deck. Waite's commentary is dense, sometimes obscure, and contains the official imagery of every card. The starting point for any serious reading of the RWS deck.

- **Pamela Colman Smith.** *Rider-Waite-Smith deck illustrations.* Rider, London, 1909.
  The deck itself is the primary source. Smith's iconography drew on the Sola Busca tarocchi, Christian mystical symbolism, theatrical staging, and her own Symbolist artistic sensibility. Read the imagery, not just the commentary.

- **Aleister Crowley.** *The Book of Thoth.* Weiser, San Francisco, 1944 (1969 reprint widely available).
  Crowley's commentary on the Thoth Tarot painted by Lady Frieda Harris. Different system from Waite's, often deeper philosophically. Essential for any reader wanting the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn line in fuller detail.

## Modern Standard References

- **Rachel Pollack.** *Seventy-Eight Degrees of Wisdom.* Aquarian Press, Wellingborough, 1980.
  The most widely-used modern textbook of Rider-Waite-Smith interpretation. Pollack synthesizes Waite's commentary with twentieth-century psychological and feminist perspectives. Read for the spread-by-spread breakdowns and the chapter on Court cards.

- **Joan Bunning.** *Learning the Tarot: A Tarot Book for Beginners.* Weiser, York Beach, 1998.
  Highly accessible card-by-card meanings with keywords. Good for cross-checking interpretations.

- **Mary K. Greer.** *21 Ways to Read a Tarot Card.* Llewellyn, St. Paul, 2006.
  Methodological — twenty-one approaches to a single card. Greer's other books, especially *Tarot for Your Self* (Newcastle, 1984), are useful for the practitioner-development side of the work.

- **Benebell Wen.** *Holistic Tarot: An Integrative Approach to Using Tarot for Personal Growth.* North Atlantic Books, Berkeley, 2015.
  Modern reference with strong card-by-card commentary and substantial appendices on numerology, astrology, and Kabbalah within tarot.

## Historical and Scholarly

- **Robert M. Place.** *The Tarot: History, Symbolism, and Divination.* Tarcher, New York, 2005.
  Among the best single-volume histories of the tarot, distinguishing what is historically attested from what is later legend. Place's research separates the Italian playing-card origins from the eighteenth-century occult readings that produced the modern divinatory tradition.

- **Sallie Nichols.** *Jung and Tarot: An Archetypal Journey.* Weiser, York Beach, 1980.
  Jungian reading of the Major Arcana as stages of individuation. Deep on the Fool's journey framing.

- **Lon Milo DuQuette.** *Understanding Aleister Crowley's Thoth Tarot.* Weiser, San Francisco, 2003.
  Accessible companion to *The Book of Thoth*. Cuts through Crowley's deliberate obscurity.

## Specialist References

- **Stuart Kaplan.** *Encyclopedia of Tarot, Volumes I–IV.* US Games Systems, Stamford, 1978–2005.
  Massive reference work cataloguing the historical decks. Indispensable for image-and-deck research.

- **Helen Farley.** *A Cultural History of Tarot: From Entertainment to Esotericism.* I.B. Tauris, London, 2009.
  Scholarly history with attention to the social and intellectual contexts in which tarot was used.

## Pamela Colman Smith — Recovery and Recognition

For decades Smith's contribution was credited only as "P.C.S." in the corner of each card. The recent recovery of her work — including the Holzer-Smith correspondence and the recognition of her Sola Busca and Symbolist influences — is documented in several places:

- **Stuart Kaplan.** *Pamela Colman Smith: The Untold Story.* US Games Systems, Stamford, 2018.
- **Catherine Wedge & Melinda Boyd Parsons.** *To All Believers — The Art of Pamela Colman Smith.* Princeton University Press, 1975.
