# Temperance (XIV)

## Image
A winged angel stands one foot on land, one foot in a small pool. Water is poured between two cups, the stream defying gravity by flowing partly upward. A solar symbol marks the angel's forehead; a triangle within a square marks the chest. A path leads to mountains behind, with a crown floating in the sun's light.

## Core Meaning
The patient mixing. Temperance is the card of measured combination — two ingredients that must be brought together in the right ratio, at the right temperature, over the right span of time. Read as integration, balance in the sense of an active process rather than a frozen point, and the quality of patience that does not hurry the alchemy.

## Upright
The angel's one foot in water, one on land, signals the marriage of conscious and unconscious, fluid and stable, what changes and what holds. The cups passing water between them are the image of long, careful work; the flow that goes partly upward is the impossible-looking part of any true integration. Tradition reads the upright card as recovery from extremes, the patient blending of incompatible-seeming parts of life, and the practice of moderation that is not abstemiousness but skilful proportion. The card is associated with the sign Sagittarius in the Golden Dawn system.

## Reversed
The mixing rushed or refused. Reversed, Temperance may indicate the polarised life that swings between the cups without ever blending — feast and fast, all-in and withdrawn — or the impatience that wants integration without the time integration takes. The shadow is the spilled water, the failed experiment.

## In a Reading
Beside the Devil, Temperance is the antidote to compulsion: the patient meeting of what was being suppressed or indulged. Beside the Star, the two cards together describe healing in two registers — slow blending and quiet hope. In a health reading, Temperance often counsels the long protocol over the dramatic intervention; in a creative reading, the steady mixing of disparate sources.

## Keywords
integration, blending, moderation, patience, alchemy, balance-as-process, Sagittarius, extremes (reversed), impatience (reversed)
