# The First House

## Header
**Cusp:** the Ascendant. **Angular.** **Natural sign:** Aries. **Natural ruler:** Mars. **Domain:** the self, the body, the appearance, the way the figure enters a room.

## Core Meaning
The doorway. The first house begins at the eastern horizon, where the sky was rising at the moment of birth. Read as the body the figure walks around in — the first impression, the personal style, the temperament that meets the world before the deeper self has been disclosed.

## Upright
Tradition reads the first house as the temperament, the body's complexion, the manner. Planets here are strongly coloured by the chart's first signature — a Mars in the first walks fast and speaks directly; a Venus in the first dresses for the room; a Saturn in the first carries the body like a slightly older person. The sign on the Ascendant is the mask that is, paradoxically, also the most honest — it is the body's actual rhythm.

## In a Chart
The Ascendant ruler (the planet ruling the sign on the cusp) is the chart's "captain of the body": its sign, house, and aspects show where the figure's main work is and what conditions the body. Tradition reads physical features by the Ascendant and its ruler — though modern practice prefers to read manner and presence rather than appearance. Planets within 5° of the Ascendant are read with extra weight, almost as another identity.

## Keywords
the body, the self, the Ascendant, the doorway, the first impression, temperament, manner
