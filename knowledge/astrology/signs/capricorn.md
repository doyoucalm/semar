# Capricorn (♑)

## Glyph
The sea-goat. The mountain animal with a fish's tail — the climbing creature whose lower half remembers the deep water.

## Header
**Element:** Earth. **Modality:** Cardinal. **Ruler:** Saturn. **Exaltation:** Mars. **Detriment:** Moon. **Fall:** Jupiter. **Body:** Bones, knees, skin, the skeletal structure. **Mythic figure:** Capricornus, the goat-fish of Sumerian Enki and Greek Pan; the elder, the mountain climber.

## Core Meaning
The long climb. Capricorn is the tenth sign, the winter solstice, the moment when the year hits bottom and begins to turn back toward light. Read as discipline, ambition, the figure whose work is to build structures that outlast their builder.

## Strength
Tradition reads Capricorn as the executive, the elder, the architect, the figure who is willing to do the unglamorous decade of work before the visible result. The cardinal earth is initiating structure: Capricorn placements found institutions, lay foundations, build the kinds of things that other signs inherit. Saturn's rulership gives Capricorn its patience with limits and its respect for time — the sign trusts what survives weather.

## Shadow
The same discipline turned cold. Capricorn placements may treat the self as a resource to be exploited, may measure worth only in achievement, may withhold warmth in the name of duty. The shadow is the figure who has climbed the mountain and discovered, at the summit, that they have forgotten how to enjoy anything.

## In a Chart
The house holding Capricorn shows where the chart is willing to do long, patient work — where structure must be built, where authority is earned rather than given, where the long view rules. Saturn's condition colours the quality of that work: a dignified Saturn makes the Capricorn placement a true elder; an afflicted Saturn makes it a workaholic. Mars in Capricorn is exalted, suggesting that ambition married to discipline is one of the chart's most reliable strengths.

## Keywords
discipline, the long climb, cardinal earth, ambition, the elder, structure, time, coldness (shadow), workaholism (shadow), achievement-as-identity (shadow)
