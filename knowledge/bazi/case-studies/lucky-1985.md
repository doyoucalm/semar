# Case Study: Lucky Surya Haryadi, 1985-05-05 09:31 WIB (Bandung)

**Day master:** 甲 (Yang Wood — the tall tree). Born in spring (month 辰, the late spring earth-month). Sect: daytime (born in mid-morning).

## Element Distribution

Counting stems and main branch elements only:
- Wood (the day master and the year stem): 2
- Earth (month branch, day branch, hour stem): 3
- Metal (month stem and year branch storage): 2 visible
- Fire (hour branch): 1
- Water (hidden in 辰 storage): present in trace

The chart is **earth-heavy** with a **wood day master**. The day master 甲 stands as a tree on a wide field of earth — a chart whose work is to grow upward against the heavy ground.

## Day Master Strength

Tradition reads 甲 in 辰 month as moderate — late spring, the earth taking over from wood. The day branch 辰 holds 乙 wood as its middle hidden stem, giving the day master a small private root. The year stem 乙 (a wood friend) adds a layer of support but is yin and somewhat soft. The chart is **slightly weak day master** in classical reading: 甲 wants reinforcement from water (resource) and additional wood (peers).

## Ten Gods Around the Day Master

- **Year stem 乙:** Rob Wealth (劫财). The wood friend, but yin — close support that can also pull at resources.
- **Year branch 丑:** holds 己 (Direct Wealth), 癸 (Direct Resource), 辛 (Direct Officer). A storage of resource, wealth, and authority — multiple themes folded into the lineage position.
- **Month stem 庚:** Seven Killings (七杀). The axe to the day master's tree — the figure's central challenge and the source of much of the chart's discipline.
- **Month branch 辰:** holds 戊 (Direct Wealth), 乙 (Rob Wealth), 癸 (Direct Resource). The career environment as a place that holds wealth, peers, and resource for the chart.
- **Day branch 辰:** the marriage palace — the same storage as month, repeated. The figure's domestic life echoes the work environment.
- **Hour stem 己:** Direct Wealth (正财). The legacy and the late-life output: wealth produced through patient work.
- **Hour branch 巳:** holds 丙 (Eating God), 庚 (Seven Killings), 戊 (Direct Wealth). A clean late-life output position with fire to express, metal to discipline, and wealth as the result.

## Interactions

- **Two 辰 branches (month + day):** **self-punishment of 辰** (自刑). Internal tension between the work life and the home life — the same earth twice, the chart's repeated theme that demands integration. The figure is asked to make peace between vocation and household.
- **巳 + 申 hidden:** no 申 directly in the chart's main branches, so the classical 巳申 dynamics do not surface strongly.
- **丑 + 辰 + 辰:** a heavy concentration of earth-storage. The chart holds many hidden stems waiting to be released by clash or by year-pillar activation.

## Shen Sha (Symbolic Stars)

- **Heavenly Noble (天乙贵人) for 甲:** 丑 and 未. Year branch 丑 carries Heavenly Noble — the figure receives help from lineage and from elders.
- **Literary Star (文昌) for 甲:** 巳. Hour branch 巳 carries Literary Star — the figure's outputs and late-life work carry the gift for words and refined expression.
- **Golden Carriage (金舆) for 甲:** 辰. Both month and day branches are 辰 — the figure is doubly carried, with comfort flowing through both work environment and marriage.
- **Canopy (华盖):** 丑 in the year branch and 辰 in the month/day branches both qualify (丑 triggers via the 巳酉丑 triad, 辰 via 申子辰). The chart has multiple Canopy resonances — suited to contemplative, scholarly, and artistic work.
- **Void (空亡) for 甲辰 旬:** 寅 and 卯 are void. Neither appears in the chart's main branches, so the voids are dormant — they may activate during luck pillars that bring 寅 or 卯.

## Reading

The chart is a yang wood tree on heavy spring earth. The dominant theme is the relationship between wood (the self) and earth (wealth, ground, the world to be worked). The Seven Killings on the month stem give the figure a serious adversary or discipline — for Lucky, the BaZi reading aligns with the founder's pattern: difficult early environment, work as discipline, the need to develop the wood's strength to handle the wealth and the office that the chart can produce.

The doubled 辰, the self-punishment, gives the figure recurrent tension between vocation and home — the same theme processed twice — and the chart's invitation is to integrate the two rather than to choose between them. The multiple Canopy stars suggest that the integrating work is contemplative as well as practical: the chart wants depth, not just output.

Literary Star in the hour pillar and Heavenly Noble in the year pillar form a quiet auspicious frame: lineage support behind the figure, refined expression ahead of them.

## Lessons for Reading

This chart illustrates several things worth carrying into other readings:
- A storage-heavy chart (multiple 辰戌丑未) does not produce its themes until something opens the storage — usually a clash or punishment year.
- Self-punishment of a doubled branch is one of the most common patterns in real charts and is rarely as dire as classical sources suggest. The figure is asked to integrate, not punished for failing to.
- Multiple shen sha do not stack mechanically — they layer. A chart with both Heavenly Noble and Canopy is a chart whose helpers tend to be elders, scholars, or contemplatives.
