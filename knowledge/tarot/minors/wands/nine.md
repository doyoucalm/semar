# Nine of Wands

## Image
A bandaged figure stands holding a wand, the other eight wands lined up like a fence behind him. He looks tense, wary. His head is bandaged from a previous wound.

## Core Meaning
The last stand. The previous battles have left marks. The questioner is tired but not broken; one more push is required. Read as resilience, the closing wall of an ordeal, weariness without surrender.

## Upright
Tradition reads the card as the long campaign nearly over, the questioner who has been worn down by repeated demands and is now bracing for what they hope is the last one. The bandage matters: this is not a fresh fighter. There is dignity in the figure's stance — and warranted suspicion of further attacks.

## Reversed
The fortifications turned paranoid, the wariness misapplied. Sometimes the figure has fortified against threats that have already passed; sometimes they have given up before the last push.

## Keywords
resilience, last stand, the worn fighter, vigilance, near-victory, the long campaign, paranoia (reversed), giving up (reversed)
