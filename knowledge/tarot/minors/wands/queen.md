# Queen of Wands

## Image
A crowned woman sits on a throne carved with lions and sunflowers. She holds a budding wand and a sunflower. A black cat sits at her feet. The sky behind is bright; the desert landscape is the suit's signature.

## Core Meaning
Fire mastered with warmth. The Queen of Wands is the figure who carries her own light — radiant, self-possessed, generous in her own brightness rather than depleted by giving. Read as confident creativity, magnetism, the matriarch of passion and enterprise.

## Upright
Tradition reads the card as the woman whose presence draws others without effort, the leader who inspires by example, the friend whose warmth is reliable because it is rooted in her own fullness. The sunflower in her hand mirrors the one carved on her throne — what she rules and what she carries are the same. The black cat at her feet is the night-side of the fire, the intuitive shadow that balances the day.

## Reversed
The radiance turned demanding, or hidden. The Queen reversed may indicate the figure whose generosity has become performative, the leader whose charisma is now a way of taking; or the opposite — the dimmed Queen, the woman whose light has been talked out of itself.

## Keywords
radiant, self-possessed, magnetic, the matriarch of fire, generous warmth, the sunflower throne, demanding (reversed), dimmed (reversed)
