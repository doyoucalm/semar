# Two of Swords

## Image
A blindfolded woman sits on a stone bench, arms crossed over her chest, two swords balanced one in each hand. A crescent moon hangs in the sky behind. The sea behind her is calm but rocky.

## Core Meaning
Stalemate or chosen blindness. The figure has refused to look — at the decision, at the conflict, at the situation. Read as truce, the deferred choice, the willed not-seeing that is sometimes wise and sometimes evasive.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the deliberate maintained balance — the cease-fire one is holding by force of will, the difficult decision postponed until more information arrives, the impasse that requires waiting. The blindfold can be wisdom or denial depending on context.

## Reversed
The blindfold lifted — for better or worse. The decision is now visible; whether the figure is ready for what she sees is the question.

## Keywords
stalemate, truce, deferred choice, the blindfold, balanced impasse, denial, decision visible (reversed)
