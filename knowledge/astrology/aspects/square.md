# Square (□)

## Header
**Glyph:** □. **Angular distance:** 90°. **Default orb:** 7°–8°. **Class:** hard (challenging).

## Core Meaning
The friction that produces work. The square places two bodies in signs of the same modality (cardinal, fixed, or mutable) but of different elements — Aries-Cancer, Taurus-Leo, Gemini-Virgo, and so on. Read as a friction in which neither principle can easily reach the other, and in which the figure must develop skill to make the two work together.

## Upright
Tradition reads the square as the most action-producing of the hard aspects. Where the opposition negotiates polarity, the square forces a decision: something must give, something must move. Squares between personal planets and outer planets are particularly formative — a Saturn square Sun, for example, often gives a figure who has had to build their selfhood against early restriction.

## Variants
The square is the central dynamic of the cardinal-fixed-mutable cross. T-squares (two planets in opposition, both squared by a third) concentrate the energy in the third planet, which becomes the focal apex. Grand crosses (four planets in two oppositions, all squared) make the chart a tightly-strung tension that the figure spends much of life learning to release skillfully.

## In a Chart
The houses involved in a square show where the friction lives. Modern practice prefers to read squares as the source of the chart's most generative work, rather than as simple "bad" aspects: the figure with a Mars square Saturn may have to learn discipline through endurance, but the discipline learned will be deep. The work of a square is to develop the muscle that turns friction into craft.

## Keywords
the friction, the forced decision, the productive tension, the cross, the T-square, the apex, the skill earned through difficulty
