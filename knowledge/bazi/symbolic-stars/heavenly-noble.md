# Heavenly Noble (天乙贵人)

## Header
**Category:** auspicious. **Anchor:** day stem. **Rule:** the day stem looks up a corresponding pair of branches anywhere in the chart.

## Lookup
| Day stem | Branches |
| --- | --- |
| 甲, 戊, 庚 | 丑, 未 |
| 乙, 己 | 子, 申 |
| 丙, 丁 | 亥, 酉 |
| 壬, 癸 | 卯, 巳 |
| 辛 | 寅, 午 |

## Core Meaning
The most respected of the auspicious stars. Heavenly Noble represents help from elders, mentors, and people of position — the figure in the chart's life who opens doors at the right time. Read as benevolence from above, the patron who appears when needed.

## In a Chart
A chart with Heavenly Noble in any of the four pillars carries a kind of background protection. The figure tends to be lucky in receiving help: a stranger gives the right advice at the right moment, a senior takes them under wing, the bureaucracy moves in their favour. The star is most active when on the day or hour branch (close to the self) or on the year branch (from family/ancestral lineage).

Heavenly Noble is read as more powerful in a chart that already has dignity and structure — the figure must be in a position to receive help. A chaotic chart with Heavenly Noble may still see help arrive, but the figure may not be ready to use it.

## When Activated
The star is activated when the corresponding branch appears in a luck pillar or annual transit. A 甲 day master who encounters a 丑 or 未 year may find unexpected support arriving in that year.

## Keywords
the patron, the mentor, help from elders, benevolence, the door opened, the auspicious presence
