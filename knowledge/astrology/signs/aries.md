# Aries (♈)

## Glyph
The ram. Two curved horns rising from a central stem — the head pushing forward, the first thrust of spring.

## Header
**Element:** Fire. **Modality:** Cardinal. **Ruler:** Mars. **Exaltation:** Sun. **Detriment:** Venus. **Fall:** Saturn. **Body:** Head and face. **Mythic figure:** the ram of the golden fleece; Ares.

## Core Meaning
The beginning. Aries opens the zodiac at the spring equinox — the moment when light overtakes dark and the seed cracks. Read as initiative, raw will, the courage that arrives before the plan does.

## Strength
Tradition reads Aries as the warrior, the pioneer, the figure who steps first into a new field. The cardinal fire is starting energy: not the steady burn of Leo or the wandering flame of Sagittarius, but the strike of the match. Aries placements give the chart the capacity to begin — to declare, to fight, to act without waiting for full information. The sign loves a clean challenge and contracts in front of bureaucracy.

## Shadow
The same fire turned destructive. Aries placements may run hot, pick fights for their own sake, charge into terrain that wanted patience instead of force. The card of Aries reversed is the figure whose energy has no banks — anger as a default state, impatience as a strategy, the wound that comes from acting before listening.

## In a Chart
The house holding Aries shows where the chart begins something on its own initiative — where the figure declares territory, where the figure cannot be persuaded to wait. Mars's condition colours how cleanly that fire moves: a dignified Mars makes the Aries placement a worker; an afflicted Mars makes it a brawler. The Sun in Aries gives the identity a pioneering shape; the Moon in Aries gives the emotional body a quick fuse and a quicker recovery.

## Keywords
beginning, initiative, the warrior, the pioneer, courage, cardinal fire, impulse, anger (shadow), impatience (shadow)
