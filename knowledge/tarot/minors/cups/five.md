# Five of Cups

## Image
A cloaked figure faces three spilled cups; two cups stand upright behind him, unseen. A river runs between him and a castle on the far side; a bridge crosses it.

## Core Meaning
Grief over what is lost while what remains is overlooked. Read as the mourning that has not yet finished, the spill that fills the foreground while the standing cups wait.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the loss being processed — divorce, bereavement, the failure of a hope. The card does not deny the grief; it points out the unseen. The two cups behind the figure are not consolation prizes; they are the future that is still possible if the figure can turn around.

## Reversed
Turning around. The figure starts to see what is still upright — the friend who is still there, the chapter that has not ended. Sometimes the reversal is the deepening of the grief, the refusal to look up.

## Keywords
grief, mourning, the spilled cups, what remains unseen, loss, turning to see (reversed), deepened grief (reversed)
