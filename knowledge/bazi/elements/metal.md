# Metal (金)

## Header
**Yang form:** 庚 gēng (the axe, the sword, the raw ore). **Yin form:** 辛 xīn (the jewel, the refined blade, the polished metal). **Season:** autumn. **Direction:** west. **Body:** lungs and large intestine, skin, nose. **Generates:** water. **Controls:** wood. **Controlled by:** fire. **Drained by:** water. **Resented by:** fire (when metal is weak fire melts it; when metal is strong fire forges it).

## Core Meaning
The element of refinement, of cutting away what does not belong. Metal is autumn, the season of harvest and pruning — the leaves let go, the wheat is separated from chaff. Read as the principle of discrimination, of justice, of the blade that distinguishes one thing from another.

## Yang Metal (庚)
Tradition reads 庚 as the axe — direct, sharp, sometimes blunt. A 庚 day master is often forthright, principled, willing to do the cutting that others avoid. The figure is the friend whose criticism is honest and the executive who makes the hard call. 庚 metal in raw form needs fire to forge it into a useful tool; without fire, it is the figure with strong instincts but no discipline.

## Yin Metal (辛)
Tradition reads 辛 as the jewel, the finished blade — metal that has already been refined. A 辛 day master is often elegant, perceptive, refined in taste; the figure whose work has the quality of polish. 辛 metal can be wounded — the jewel is precious and easily marked — but well-cared-for 辛 produces some of the chart's most beautiful work.

## When Strong
Strong metal (born in autumn, surrounded by earth and metal) wants something to cut. Fire is the remedy: the discipline that turns raw metal into a sword, the work that gives the figure something to refine. Strong metal without fire is the figure who has the capacity for excellence and nothing to apply it to.

## When Weak
Weak metal (born in spring or summer, surrounded by wood and fire) needs earth to generate it and metal-friends to brace it. Weak metal is the figure whose discrimination has been overrun — too many things to cut, not enough strength to make the cut clean. The remedy is rest and clear lines of authority.

## Keywords
autumn, refinement, cutting, justice, the axe (yang), the jewel (yin), discrimination, pruning, harvest, harshness (shadow)
