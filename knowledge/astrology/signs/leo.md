# Leo (♌)

## Glyph
The lion's mane and tail — a flowing curl. The shape of radiating from a centre.

## Header
**Element:** Fire. **Modality:** Fixed. **Ruler:** Sun. **Detriment:** Saturn / Uranus (modern). **Body:** Heart, spine, upper back. **Mythic figure:** the Nemean lion; Apollo; the king.

## Core Meaning
The heart shining. Leo is the fifth sign, the height of summer, the moment when the year burns most fully. Read as creative expression, sovereignty, the figure who has the courage to be seen as themselves.

## Strength
Tradition reads Leo as the performer, the king, the parent, the figure whose generosity is the warmth of the sun on a cool morning. The fixed fire is steady radiance — not Aries's spark or Sagittarius's wandering flame, but the long burn that holds the centre of the room. Leo placements love to make — children, art, gestures, courtship — and they love the appreciation that comes back. The sign at its best is dignity that gives others permission to shine.

## Shadow
The same fire turned vain. Leo placements may need applause more than they need truth, may confuse being central with being beloved, may build a stage so large that no real conversation can happen on it. The shadow is the figure whose pride has eaten the heart it was supposed to express.

## In a Chart
The house holding Leo shows where the chart wants to create and be appreciated for the creation — where the figure performs, where children and lovers and projects gather. The Sun's condition is the master key: the Sun's sign, house, and aspects show whether Leo's radiance is steady or whether it flickers. The Moon in Leo wants emotional acknowledgement; the Ascendant in Leo gives the body a regal carriage.

## Keywords
the heart, sovereignty, fixed fire, creative expression, the performer, generosity, vanity (shadow), need for applause (shadow)
