# Five of Wands

## Image
Five young men spar with wands raised. Their motions are confused — some are striking, some are defending, some look uncertain about what they are doing. The scene reads more as practice or melee than as serious combat.

## Core Meaning
Competition, disagreement, the friction of opposed wills. The figures are not enemies — they are players in the same field whose interests temporarily collide. Read as productive conflict, scrappy competition, the energy of contest.

## Upright
The wands clash but do not break. Tradition reads the card as the team meeting where everyone speaks at once, the marketplace of competing ventures, the rivalry that sharpens rather than wounds. The contest is real but the stakes are bounded.

## Reversed
The scrap has hardened into actual conflict, or has been suppressed and now boils underneath. Sometimes the figure has stopped engaging — has stepped out of the field where contest would have been useful.

## Keywords
competition, productive friction, sparring, contest, disagreement, opposed wills, suppressed conflict (reversed)
