# Mars (♂)

## Glyph
A circle with an arrow rising from it — the shield with the spear pointed outward. The warrior's emblem.

## Header
**Domicile:** Aries, Scorpio (traditional). **Exaltation:** Capricorn (28°). **Detriment:** Libra, Taurus. **Fall:** Cancer. **Sect:** nocturnal (lesser malefic in night, greater malefic in day). **Mythic figure:** Ares; Mars; Tyr; Skanda.

## Core Meaning
The capacity for action and assertion. Mars is what the figure does with anger, desire, and effort — the function that cuts, drives, fights, and finishes. Read as the will, the muscle, the boundary's defender.

## Upright
Tradition reads Mars as the warrior, the surgeon, the athlete, the figure whose energy gets things done. The sign holding Mars describes the texture of the will — fast or slow, hot or cold, public or private. The house holding Mars describes where the figure exerts force and where conflict tends to occur. A dignified Mars makes the figure capable of saying no, capable of finishing a task even when bored, capable of defending what they love.

## Afflicted
The same force turned destructive. A Mars in detriment, fall, or hard aspect to malefics may indicate a figure whose anger lacks a useful direction — irritability, accidents, the recurrent fight, the boundary that snaps too late or breaks the wrong thing. Tradition reads Mars as the "lesser malefic" — capable of cutting through what needs to be cut through, but capable also of cutting indiscriminately.

## In a Chart
Mars's sign answers "how does this person want?" — Mars in Aries pushes, Mars in Scorpio strategises, Mars in Pisces dissolves. Mars's aspects to Venus describe the relationship between desire and value; Mars's aspects to Saturn describe the relationship between drive and discipline (often the muscle that does long work, or the impulse repeatedly blocked); Mars's aspects to Pluto describe the relationship between personal will and deeper forces.

## Keywords
will, action, anger, desire, the warrior, drive, courage, irritability (shadow), accident-prone (shadow), violence (shadow)
