# Uranus (♅)

## Glyph
The H of Herschel (the planet's discoverer in 1781) or, in older convention, a circle with an upright arrow — the spirit electrified.

## Header
**Modern ruler of:** Aquarius. **Discovered:** 1781 by William Herschel. **Orbital period:** ~84 years. **Sect:** ambivalent; modern astrologers treat as a generational outer planet with personal salience when aspecting personal planets or angles. **Mythic figure:** Ouranos, the sky-father; Prometheus the firebringer.

## Core Meaning
The shock that liberates. Uranus is the function that breaks the form when the form has grown too small — sudden insight, technological leap, the lightning strike that ends one regime and begins another. Read as awakening, individuation, the part of the figure that cannot finally be tamed.

## Upright
Modern tradition reads Uranus as the inventor, the rebel, the figure who sees what the system has not yet allowed itself to see. The sign holding Uranus is generational — everyone born within about seven years shares it — but the house describes where the chart's awakenings tend to come, and the aspects describe what is disrupted into freedom. A well-integrated Uranus gives the figure the capacity to think for themselves, to leave a system that has failed them, to live with the original rather than the inherited.

## Afflicted
The same lightning turned destructive. A Uranus in hard aspect to lights or angles may indicate a figure whose life pattern is rupture without integration — relationships abandoned at the first constraint, careers shattered to avoid commitment, the contrarian whose freedom has become another prison. The shadow Uranus rebels indiscriminately.

## In a Chart
Uranus's house is where shocks reliably arrive — and where, over the long arc, the chart's most original contribution lives. Aspects to personal planets describe what is wired to the awakening: Uranus to the Sun shocks the identity; Uranus to Venus disrupts the love patterns; Uranus to Mars makes the will electric. The Uranus opposition (~age 42) is the classic midlife threshold — the point where the inherited life is either re-chosen or set down.

## Keywords
awakening, the rebel, the inventor, individuation, freedom, the shock, lightning, rupture (shadow), contrarianism (shadow), destabilisation (shadow)
