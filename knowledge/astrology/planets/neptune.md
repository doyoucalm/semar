# Neptune (♆)

## Glyph
The trident of the sea-god, a cross at the base. The fork of waves.

## Header
**Modern ruler of:** Pisces. **Discovered:** 1846 by Galle and d'Arrest from Le Verrier's prediction. **Orbital period:** ~165 years. **Mythic figure:** Poseidon; Neptune; the dreaming sea; the dissolving god.

## Core Meaning
The dissolution that opens the imaginal. Neptune is the function by which boundaries soften — between self and other, between dream and waking, between the world as it is and the world as longed-for. Read as mysticism, art, compassion, illusion — the part of the figure that can no longer maintain hard separation.

## Upright
Modern tradition reads Neptune as the mystic, the artist, the saint, the healer, the figure whose feeling reaches what other functions cannot. The house holding Neptune describes where the chart is most porous, where the figure is willing to give without strict accounting, where the imaginal lives. A well-held Neptune brings access to beauty, meaning, and the kind of compassion that touches another's suffering without needing to fix it.

## Afflicted
The same dissolution turned to confusion. Neptune in hard aspect to lights or personal planets may indicate a figure prone to deception (of self or by others), addiction, victim/saviour patterns, or the long fog that prevents action. Tradition watches for the lover whose beloved is a projection, the believer whose belief is escape, the helper whose helping erases the self.

## In a Chart
Neptune's house is where the figure cannot hold a clean line — and also where they can dissolve into something larger than the self when conditions are right. Aspects to personal planets describe what is dissolved or sensitised: Neptune to the Sun softens the identity; Neptune to the Moon makes the feeling oceanic; Neptune to Venus romanticises love; Neptune to Mercury can give either poetic genius or chronic vagueness. The Neptune square (~age 42) is read alongside the Uranus opposition as part of the midlife dissolution.

## Keywords
dissolution, the dream, compassion, the artist, mysticism, the sea, illusion (shadow), addiction (shadow), confusion (shadow), saviour/victim patterns (shadow)
