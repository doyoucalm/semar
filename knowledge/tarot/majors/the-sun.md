# The Sun (XIX)

## Image
A great sun with a serene human face shines in a clear blue sky. A naked child rides a white horse forward; the horse is unbridled. The child holds a red banner aloft and wears a single red feather and a crown of sunflowers. Behind, a low stone wall and four tall sunflowers turn their faces toward the sun.

## Core Meaning
Clarity, vitality, the daylight after the night-walk. The Sun is the card of seeing plainly, of the joy that does not depend on hiding the difficult, of the simple presence of warmth. Read as success, vitality, the moment the question stops being shadowy and becomes obvious. The child on the horse — naked, unbridled, banner held high — is the figure who has come through the Moon's country into the morning.

## Upright
The horse is white, like the Death card's horse — but this rider does not carry the banner of ending. The Sun's child is the same Fool from the beginning, returned with what was learned: he can ride bareback now, he is not afraid of the sun, he carries the banner forward. Tradition reads the upright card as success in projects, recovery in health, joy in relationship, the public good outcome. The wall behind is the threshold crossed; the sunflowers are the witnesses who have turned their faces to follow the same light.

## Reversed
The sun obscured. Reversed, the Sun may indicate the joy that is being damped — not gone, just clouded — by self-doubt, by the failure to receive what is being offered, by the conviction that one's good fortune is somehow undeserved. On another side, the reversal can show the false brightness that papers over genuine difficulty: the smile in the photograph of the failing marriage.

## In a Reading
Beside the Moon, the daylight that has succeeded the night. Beside the Star, two registers of hope — the Star quiet, the Sun frank. In a question about outcome, the Sun is one of the brightest indicators in the deck; in a question about a relationship, it points to the kind of love that does not have to hide. In a child or family reading it is often literal.

## Keywords
vitality, joy, clarity, success, daylight, the unbridled horse, recovery, public good, dampened joy (reversed), false brightness (reversed)
