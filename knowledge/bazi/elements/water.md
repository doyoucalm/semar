# Water (水)

## Header
**Yang form:** 壬 rén (the great river, the ocean). **Yin form:** 癸 guǐ (the rain, the dew, the morning mist). **Season:** winter. **Direction:** north. **Body:** kidneys and bladder, bones, ears, reproductive organs. **Generates:** wood. **Controls:** fire. **Controlled by:** earth. **Drained by:** wood. **Resented by:** earth (when water is weak earth dams it; when water is strong earth shapes its banks).

## Core Meaning
The element of depth, of return, of flow. Water is winter, the season when the energies retreat below the surface and gather. Read as the principle of intelligence, of memory, of the wisdom that comes from going down rather than up.

## Yang Water (壬)
Tradition reads 壬 as the great river — the wide flow that runs without hurry but does not stop. A 壬 day master is often intelligent, perceptive, strategically patient; the figure who takes the long view and outwaits the obstacle. 壬 water shapes the landscape over time. Without earth to give it banks, 壬 floods; with earth, it becomes navigable.

## Yin Water (癸)
Tradition reads 癸 as the mist and the dew — water that enters every space without forcing. A 癸 day master is often sensitive, intuitive, attentive to subtleties that others miss. The figure tends to be gentle, contemplative, drawn to study, art, or healing. 癸 water is more fragile than 壬 — easily disturbed, easily evaporated by heat — but it reaches places no other element can.

## When Strong
Strong water (born in winter, surrounded by metal and water) wants to move. Wood is the remedy: a project, a study, a creative outlet that channels the flow into something. Earth controls and contains it. Strong water without channel becomes the figure whose intelligence is dispersed across everything and concentrated on nothing.

## When Weak
Weak water (born in summer, surrounded by fire and earth) needs metal to generate it and water-friends to brace. Weak water is the figure whose intuition is unreliable, whose memory is fragmented, who runs dry under pressure. The remedy is replenishment: rest, silence, return to source.

## Keywords
winter, depth, intelligence, memory, flow, the river (yang), the mist (yin), wisdom, the long view, dispersal (shadow), evaporation under pressure (shadow)
