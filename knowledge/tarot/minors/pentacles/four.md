# Four of Pentacles

## Image
A crowned figure sits on a low stool, holding a pentacle close to his chest with both arms. Another pentacle balances on his head; two more are pinned under his feet. A city is in the background.

## Core Meaning
Holding too tight. The figure has accumulated enough — but the posture is defensive, gripping, fearful. Read as the miser's pose, the over-secured life, the wealth that has become identity.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as security cherished to the point of rigidity — the financial caution that has hardened into clinging, the property held so tightly that it cannot be enjoyed. The card is not always a warning; sometimes the figure has rational reasons to grip. But the held pentacles are not circulating; they are not growing.

## Reversed
The grip loosened — generosity returning, the willingness to spend, the wealth that begins to move again. Or the grip tightening further into pathological hoarding.

## Keywords
holding tight, the miser, defensive wealth, security as identity, the unmoved pentacle, ungenerous, loosening (reversed)
