# The High Priestess (II)

## Image
A veiled figure sits between two pillars — one black (Boaz), one white (Jachin) — drawn from the Solomonic temple. A crescent moon rests at her feet, a solar cross at her chest, and a horned-disc crown sits above her. A partly-hidden scroll bearing the word **TORA** is held in her lap. The veil behind her is patterned with pomegranates; it screens an unseen ocean.

## Core Meaning
The knowing that does not speak. Where the Magician acts, the Priestess waits and listens. She is the still pool between the pillars of opposites; the figure who has read what is on the scroll but does not unroll it for everyone. Tradition reads her as intuition, the unconscious, sacred silence, and the inner teacher whose lessons cannot be transmitted in words.

## Upright
The Priestess is the council of the inner voice. She is associated with the Moon, with reflection rather than emission of light, and with knowledge that arrives through stillness — the dream, the hunch, the body that knows before the mind does. The two pillars are not warring opposites she chooses between; she sits in the gap that holds them together. The pomegranate veil traces back to Persephone — the woman who knows the underworld and has returned, but does not chatter about what is on the other side.

## Reversed
Intuition discounted, or intuition inflated. Reversed, the Priestess may indicate a refusal to consult one's own knowing — the deference to outside authorities when the answer is already arrived at internally. The shadow form is also withdrawal: silence as evasion rather than as listening, secrecy as control. Hidden information, including from oneself.

## In a Reading
Beside the Magician, the conscious craftsman and the silent knower form a complete pair. Beside the Empress, the inner voice precedes the outer flowering. In the advice position, the Priestess speaks against rushing: the answer is already there, beneath the surface of the day. In a relationship reading, she points to what is not being said out loud.

## Keywords
intuition, the unconscious, sacred silence, inner knowing, the Moon, mystery, reflection, secrecy (reversed), discounted intuition (reversed)

## Sources

- Rachel Pollack, *Seventy-Eight Degrees of Wisdom* (Aquarian Press, 1980) — places the High Priestess in the Persephone / Isis lineage.
- Mary K. Greer, *The Complete Book of Tarot Reversals* (Llewellyn, 2002) — for the inflated-intuition reversal nuance.
