# Two of Wands

## Image
A figure in a red robe stands on a parapet looking out over land and sea. He holds a small globe in one hand; one wand is fixed to the wall, the other he grips loosely. Lilies and roses are carved into the stones.

## Core Meaning
The view from the threshold. The first venture has been launched; the question now is whether to consolidate where one is or sail further. Read as planning, the wider horizon, the choice between known territory and the next move.

## Upright
The held globe is the small version of the world the figure is considering reaching for. Tradition reads the card as strategic vision, the moment after the first success when the question of scope arises. Power has been earned but not yet exercised at the next scale.

## Reversed
Hesitation at the threshold, or overreach without preparation — the globe held but the journey unplanned. Sometimes the figure refuses the larger move out of comfort with the parapet.

## Keywords
planning, threshold, vision, scope, the next move, strategic patience, hesitation (reversed)
