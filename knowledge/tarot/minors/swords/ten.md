# Ten of Swords

## Image
A figure lies face-down on the ground at the water's edge, ten swords stuck along his back. A black sky covers the upper half of the image; at the horizon, gold light is breaking — dawn coming.

## Core Meaning
The rock-bottom mental defeat. The worst has happened — the betrayal, the loss, the collapse of the position. Read as the end of a difficult chapter, often experienced as catastrophic, but containing the seed of dawn on the horizon.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the end of the suit's hard journey: there are no more swords to be added. The card is paradoxically reassuring — the bottom has been hit, and only the rising remains. The gold horizon is the central detail. The figure does not move yet; he is allowed his exhaustion.

## Reversed
The rising slow but begun, or the figure refusing to rise — staying down past the time when staying down is honest. Sometimes the reversal is partial recovery, the inability to fully lift but the willingness to try.

## Keywords
rock bottom, defeat, the worst happened, dawn on the horizon, exhaustion, end of the cycle, slow rising (reversed), refusing rise (reversed)
