一
Chapter One · Soul

魂Soul of the Codex

Before any pixel, this is the gravity the work bends around.

Codex is not an app. Codex is a vessel for a sage path that has been forming since a boy in Bandung first read 三國演義 and recognized himself in the strategist. It is the place where eight wisdom traditions meet, and the lens through which a Profile 3/5 Universalizer hands his life experiments to the people who arrive asking for a map.

Manifesto · Lima Kalimat

Why this exists.

Pertama — wisdom traditions tidak terpisah. Tarot, BaZi, I Ching, 觀音靈籤, Vedic, Western astro, Human Design, Jawa-Bali wewaran — semua adalah lensa berbeda terhadap realitas yang sama. Sage di abad ke-13 tidak memilih satu — dia sintesiskan.

Kedua — Codex bukan menjawab pertanyaan. Codex memberi mata untuk membaca sendiri. Setiap user adalah Sudhana yang datang ke gurunya — bukan untuk dijawab, tapi untuk diberi peta.

Ketiga — Codex personal. Natal chart user terjahit ke setiap reading. Tidak ada horoskop generik. Tidak ada nasihat untuk "semua orang Taurus".

Keempat — Codex sabar. Pola muncul setelah berbulan-bulan, bukan dalam satu sesi. Recognition is the gift.

Kelima — Codex hormat. Sakralitas dijaga. Ini bukan game, bukan content, bukan algoritma engagement. Ini adalah meja tulis biksu di kuil pegunungan, malam hari, lentera menyala.

Codex tidak memberi jawaban. Codex memberi mata.

名The Name System

Three names, one soul. Each carries a different weight; each shows up in a different room.

Internal codename · for the team

Semar

Punakawan dari pewayangan Jawa. Semar adalah penjelmaan Sang Hyang Ismaya dalam wujud abdi rendah — keilahian yang menyamar jadi kesederhanaan. Pakai nama ini di dalam tim, di repo, di GitHub, di doa.

When to use: internal code, repo names, file metadata, working titles, voice memos to self.

Product name · for the world

天人合一 · Codex

Tiānrén Héyī — Heaven and Human as One. The classical Confucian-Daoist principle that the practitioner and the cosmos are not separate. Codex (Latin: bound book) anchors the metaphor: this is a notebook the user keeps for a lifetime.

When to use: the app's name in app stores, splash screens, written communications to users, public-facing pages.

Written as: 天人合一 Codex (with em-space or middle dot) — never just "Codex" in marketing, never just "天人合一" without the Latin pair.

Pigment logic — when each color speaks

Always

  • Ink Indigo for app background. Always. There is no light mode.
  • Ember Gold for the one thing on screen that asks to be seen — a primary CTA, the day's pillar, the synthesis verdict.
  • Vermillion only for ritual moments — opening a reading, completing a 108x chant, marking a sacred date. It should feel rare.
  • Parchment Cream when content asks to be read in long form — a Kuan Yin verse, the synthesis essay, an exported report.
  • Jade for streaks, progress, alive-state indicators.
  • Bronze for timestamps, captions, fine print — anything the eye should skim over.

Never

  • No purples. The mystical-galaxy-purple is the visual cliché this brand exists to refuse.
  • No pure white (#FFFFFF). Always Parchment 50 or warmer. The eye must rest like at temple.
  • No neon. No saturated blues. No magic-portal cyans.
  • No gradients with more than two stops. Two-tone candle-glow only.
  • Never two accent colors on the same screen. Ember or Vermillion — never both shouting.
  • Never red-for-error. Use Vermillion sparingly and only for sacred fire, never for system warnings.

Chapter Eight · Voice

聲Voice & Tone

Sage voice. Multilingual by default. Warm but never cute, precise but never cold, sacred but never preachy.

Six rules of voice

  1. 一 Observe, do not prescribe. "Today is a 子午沖 day" — not "You should rest today." The reader decides what the observation means for them.
  2. 二 Carry the original tongue. Don't translate 食神 to "Eating God"; show both. The original word holds meaning the English can't.
  3. 三 No "vibes." No "energies." No emoji. If you would not write it in a temple manuscript, do not write it in Codex.
  4. 四 Short. Then shorter. Sage voice prunes. Three short sentences beat one explained one. The reader fills the silence with their own meaning.
  5. 五 Never flatter the reader. No "you're a special soul." No "ancient wisdom unlocked." Treat the reader like a fellow practitioner, not a customer.
  6. 六 Refuse certainty. "This may mean" not "This means." "Often" not "always." Divination is the art of suggesting; we are not oracles for hire.

Voice samples, in four tongues

Bahasa · daily check-in
"Hari ini 庚午. Logam Yang ketemu kuda. Sinyalnya: jaga kata di rapat sore. Yang ingin meledak, biarkan habis di dalam dulu."
Hangat. Spesifik. Tidak menggurui. Akhiran ringan, bukan perintah.
English · synthesis output
"Three systems point to the same hinge: timing. The Magician (you have the tools), Hex 64 Wei Chi (not yet complete), Verse 34 (wait for spring). Prepare, do not launch."
Precise. Names the systems by name. Verdict in plain words, no padding.
中文 · 流日 reading
「今日庚午，金克木。日主受冲，宜守不宜攻。語要慎，行要緩。」
Classical compression. No filler particles. Reads like a temple log.
Sanskrit · ritual opening
ॐ मणि पद्मे हूँ — May this reading serve the awakening of the one who reads.
Only inside ritual moments. Never in UI buttons or system messages.

Microcopy — what each surface sounds like

Chapter Eleven · Anti-Patterns

非What Codex Is Not

A brand is defined as much by its refusals as its choices. These are the visual and tonal directions Codex will never drift toward, however popular the trend.

Never look like

  • Co-Star — aggressive sans-serif, neon yellow, sass-as-product. We are not punks.
  • Sanctuary / Pattern apps — pastel gradients, friendly bubbles, gamified daily streaks. We are not casual.
  • Labyrinthos modern — clean minimal cards, white space SaaS aesthetic. We are not a productivity tool.
  • The Pattern — purple-magenta gradient mysticism, vague generalities. We are not a vibe.
  • AI-tarot apps — Midjourney-style mystical art, glowing portals, "ancient wisdom unlocked." We are not a fantasy.

Always look like

  • A 13th-century manuscript that survived in a temple library, photographed in ambient candlelight.
  • An observatory notebook from a Yuan-dynasty astronomer who also studied Vedic charts.
  • The personal logbook of a wandering sage who never stayed long enough to call any tradition his own.
  • The closing chop of a master who has nothing to prove and nothing to sell.

Specific bans

No emoji
∅ 🔮 ✨ 🌙
Use the Chinese character, the trigram, or the line-art glyph. Emoji are loud; Codex is quiet.
No Inter, no Roboto, no SF
∅ system sans
A practitioner's notebook does not look like a settings panel.
No light mode toggle
∅ ☀ / ☾
Codex is always dark, always temple. The candle does not flick on and off.
No streak gamification
∅ "you're on fire 🔥"
Practice is honored, not rewarded. The mala bead counter glows; it does not shout.
No AI-mystical art
∅ Midjourney sage
Use real woodblock, real calligraphy, or hand-drawn line-art. Generated imagery is forbidden in the brand layer.
No certainty
∅ "Your destiny is..."
"May suggest." "Often indicates." "Frequently appears with." Codex offers; it never decrees.
