# Venus (♀)

## Glyph
A circle above a cross — the spirit over the matter, or the hand-mirror. The oldest of the planetary symbols still in common use.

## Header
**Domicile:** Taurus, Libra. **Exaltation:** Pisces (27°). **Detriment:** Scorpio, Aries. **Fall:** Virgo. **Sect:** nocturnal (lesser benefic in day, greater benefic in night by traditional reckoning). **Mythic figure:** Aphrodite; Inanna; Ishtar; Lakshmi.

## Core Meaning
The capacity for love and beauty. Venus is what the figure values, what they reach for in pleasure, how they relate to art and to lovers. Read as the function that brings things together by attraction rather than by force — the magnet, the smile, the room arranged for company.

## Upright
Tradition reads Venus as the lover, the artist, the friend, the figure whose hands shape harmony. The sign holding Venus describes the taste — what kind of beauty the figure recognises and is drawn to. The house holding Venus describes where pleasure and partnership most readily find the figure. A dignified Venus brings ease into the chart: relationships work, money flows, the body enjoys itself.

## Afflicted
The same Venus turned to compulsion. A Venus in detriment, fall, or hard aspect to malefics may indicate a figure whose love runs sour — partners chosen for need rather than for love, beauty pursued at the cost of substance, pleasure that turns to greed or jealousy. The shadow Venus may hoard or seduce or punish through withdrawal of approval.

## In a Chart
Venus's sign is the answer to "what does this person find beautiful?" — Venus in Taurus loves the sensual, Venus in Libra the harmonious, Venus in Capricorn the well-built. Venus's aspects to the Moon describe the emotional life of love; Venus's aspects to Mars describe the chemistry between attraction and desire; Venus's aspects to Saturn describe the work of long-term partnership. Venus never travels more than 48° from the Sun.

## Keywords
love, beauty, value, art, pleasure, attraction, partnership, vanity (shadow), seduction-as-control (shadow), envy (shadow)
