# Ten of Pentacles

## Image
Within a stone archway, an old man with a long beard sits with two large hounds. A couple stand in middle ground; a child plays at the old man's feet. Ten pentacles are arranged around the scene in the pattern of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. Banners and stonework suggest a wealthy estate.

## Core Meaning
Family wealth, lineage, the dynasty. The card depicts the long material work of generations — the estate that has been built by ancestors and is now being passed down. Read as the family that holds together, the inheritance, the deep belonging that comes from a place with a history.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the prosperous family, the wedding into a long line, the wealth that comes with continuity rather than with the windfall. The old man and the dogs suggest the patriarch who has seen the generations turn; the child playing is the future receiving what the past built. The Tree of Life pattern is Waite's hermetic layering: the wealth is also spiritual.

## Reversed
The family broken, or the inheritance contested. Sometimes the wealth has become the family's wound — the estate that nobody wants, the legacy that demands a price the next generation refuses to pay.

## Keywords
family wealth, lineage, the dynasty, deep belonging, inheritance, the long line, broken family (reversed), contested inheritance (reversed)
