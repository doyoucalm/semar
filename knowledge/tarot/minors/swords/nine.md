# Nine of Swords

## Image
A figure sits up in bed, head in hands, weeping or in despair. Nine swords hang on the wall behind. The bed is carved with a scene of one figure attacking another. A quilt of roses and zodiac signs covers the lower body.

## Core Meaning
The night terror. The card depicts the nightmare hour — anxiety in its sharpest form, grief at 3 AM, the catastrophising mind. Read as worry, insomnia, the suffering that does not have a witness.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the dark night of the mind — depression, anxiety, the grief that wakes one in the small hours. The carved bed and patterned quilt suggest this is not a new bed; this is recurring. The card carries compassion: the swords on the wall do not literally pierce the figure, and the morning will come.

## Reversed
The first signs of relief, the morning's first light; or the further descent — the panic that has spread to daylight, the depression with no observed bottom.

## Keywords
night terror, anxiety, the 3 AM hour, grief, insomnia, despair, relief beginning (reversed), deeper descent (reversed)
