# Ace of Swords

## Image
A hand from a cloud holds a single upright sword. A crown rests atop the sword, hung with laurel and palm. Six yods fall around it. Mountains rise below.

## Core Meaning
The clear cut. A decisive intellectual moment — the truth named, the decision made, the breakthrough that cuts through fog. Read as mental clarity, the right word, the sword of discernment newly drawn.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the moment one finally sees what is going on, the verdict named, the precise idea that organises the chaos. The crown signals legitimate authority: clarity earned, not merely asserted. Often a beginning that involves an act of mind — a contract signed, a manifesto written, a truth spoken out loud.

## Reversed
The sword used wrong. Confusion, the wrong word said, the decision made on bad information, or the clarity weaponised — truth as cruelty.

## Keywords
clear cut, decisive clarity, the truth named, breakthrough, mental beginning, the right word, confusion (reversed), cruelty (reversed)
