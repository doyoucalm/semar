# Earth (土)

## Header
**Yang form:** 戊 wù (the mountain, the great plateau). **Yin form:** 己 jǐ (the field, the garden soil, the wet earth). **Season:** the four seasonal transitions (each marked by an earth-month). **Direction:** centre. **Body:** spleen and stomach, muscles, mouth. **Generates:** metal. **Controls:** water. **Controlled by:** wood. **Drained by:** metal. **Resented by:** wood (when earth is weak wood opens it; when earth is strong wood drains too slowly).

## Core Meaning
The element of stability, of holding. Earth sits at the centre of the five-element cycle — the ground on which the other elements stand. Read as the principle of containment, of long memory, of what holds things together over time.

## Yang Earth (戊)
Tradition reads 戊 as the mountain — solid, immovable, watching over the long view. A 戊 day master is often grounded, dependable, slow to anger and slow to forgive, the figure who keeps promises across decades. 戊 earth is the foundation everyone builds on but few notice; the figure is often underestimated until the moment something needs to be carried.

## Yin Earth (己)
Tradition reads 己 as the cultivated field — soil that has been worked, that can grow what is planted. A 己 day master is often nurturing, careful, observant of small needs. The figure is the friend who notices when someone hasn't eaten, the host who has thought through every detail. 己 earth is more flexible than 戊 — the soil that adjusts to the season.

## When Strong
Strong earth (born in earth-months 辰戌丑未, or surrounded by earth and fire) needs to be opened. Wood is the remedy: a tree that breaks up the field, a project that gives the earth something to do. Strong earth without wood becomes the figure who has built so much containment that nothing new can enter.

## When Weak
Weak earth (born in spring when wood is strong, or surrounded by wood and water) needs fire to warm it and earth-friends to brace it. Weak earth that has been over-worked feels exhausted: the caretaker depleted by giving, the body that has run out of stamina.

## Keywords
the centre, stability, holding, the long memory, the mountain (yang), the cultivated field (yin), nurture, the foundation, exhaustion (shadow)
