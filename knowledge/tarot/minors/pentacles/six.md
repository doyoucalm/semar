# Six of Pentacles

## Image
A well-dressed merchant holds an even scale in one hand and drops coins into the hands of two kneeling beggars with the other. Six pentacles are arranged in the air.

## Core Meaning
Giving with measure. The figure has wealth and is sharing it — fairly, according to a standard. Read as generosity in its institutional form, philanthropy, the just distribution.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the giver who gives appropriately — neither hoarding nor squandering, neither rejecting requests nor showering them indiscriminately. The scale matters; this is not charity by impulse but by judgement. The card can also indicate the position of the receiver — the questioner who is, in the moment, the one with hands open.

## Reversed
The scale tipped. Generosity that is actually control — strings attached to the gift, debt accumulating in the receiver. Or the giving that has become exhausted, the well that needs to be refilled before more can be drawn.

## Keywords
giving, philanthropy, fair distribution, the even scale, generosity measured, giver or receiver, conditional gift (reversed), depleted giver (reversed)
