# Jupiter (♃)

## Glyph
A stylised numeral 4, or the Greek zeta. The thunderbolt of Zeus, simplified.

## Header
**Domicile:** Sagittarius, Pisces (traditional). **Exaltation:** Cancer (15°). **Detriment:** Gemini, Virgo. **Fall:** Capricorn. **Sect:** diurnal (greater benefic in day). **Mythic figure:** Zeus; Jupiter; Marduk; Brihaspati the guru.

## Core Meaning
The expansive function. Jupiter is generosity, faith, the search for meaning, the part of the figure that wants more — more knowledge, more travel, more belief, more good fortune. Read as the function that opens, that makes room, that returns from the journey with a story.

## Upright
Tradition reads Jupiter as the priest, the philosopher, the teacher, the figure whose presence brings benefit. The sign holding Jupiter describes the kind of meaning the figure pursues. The house holding Jupiter describes where good fortune most reliably finds them, where they can be generous without strain, where they are willing to study. The greater benefic, dignified Jupiter, blesses what it touches — though it can also bless to excess.

## Afflicted
The same generosity turned to excess. A Jupiter in detriment, fall, or hard aspect may indicate a figure who promises more than they can deliver, expands beyond what the system can hold, mistakes faith for proof, or whose appetites overrun the body. Tradition watches for the inflation that turns trust into recklessness.

## In a Chart
Jupiter's sign answers "where does this person find meaning?" — Jupiter in Sagittarius in the framework, Jupiter in Cancer in the family, Jupiter in Pisces in the imaginal. Jupiter's house shows where the figure grows. Jupiter's aspects describe what is augmented: Jupiter to the Sun magnifies the identity; Jupiter to the Moon makes the feeling generous; Jupiter to Saturn (the great cycle of about 20 years) describes the rhythm of expansion and consolidation in the figure's public life.

## Keywords
expansion, faith, meaning, generosity, the philosopher, fortune, the journey, excess (shadow), grandiosity (shadow), broken promises (shadow)
