# Destructions / Breaks (六破)

## Header
**Type:** branch break pair. **Pairs:** 子酉 / 丑辰 / 寅亥 (note: 寅亥 is also a six-combination) / 卯午 / 巳申 / 戌未. **Effect:** mild damage between branches; the least intense of the branch interactions.

## Core Meaning
The pairs that crack rather than shatter. Destructions (or "breaks") are a minor branch interaction — the chip in the pottery rather than the smashed vase. Read as small obstacles, minor friction, the project that has setbacks but is not derailed.

## In a Chart
Destructions are the lightest of the negative branch interactions. Many modern practitioners give them little or no weight, treating them as a footnote to the more important six clashes, six combinations, and three punishments. Classical sources, however, list them, and they sometimes refine readings between branches that otherwise seem neutral.

A destruction between year and month suggests minor friction in early environment. Between day and hour, small troubles with children or close partner. Between month and day, a slow grinding in the figure's relationship to their work environment.

## In Practice
Destructions are most useful in horary-style readings — short-term forecasts, when a year or month branch breaks a chart branch and produces a minor but specific event. For long-term natal interpretation, they are usually background detail unless reinforced by other interactions.

Note: 寅亥 appears in both the destruction list and the six-combination list. Classical lineages differ on how to resolve this; some treat the combination as dominant, some give the destruction precedence under certain conditions. Pragmatic practice: the combination is the primary reading; the destruction is a hint of underlying friction.

## Keywords
the minor crack, small obstacles, the background friction, the chip in the pottery
