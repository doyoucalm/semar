# Three of Wands

## Image
A figure stands with his back to us on a cliff, looking out over a sea where three ships are sailing. Three wands rise behind him; he holds the central one. The sky is gold.

## Core Meaning
The ships have been sent and are sailing. The venture is no longer planned; it is underway, and the questioner is in the position of the one who waits for what was sent forward to come back. Read as expansion already happening, foresight rewarded, the long view.

## Upright
This is the card of established progress. The figure has earned the elevated view; his vision has been put into motion. Tradition reads it as the entrepreneur whose first investments are bearing fruit, the leader whose project has scaled, the traveller whose long plan is paying out. There is patience here, and confidence.

## Reversed
Plans delayed, or the ships sailing to the wrong port. The figure waits and the horizon stays empty. Sometimes self-doubt about whether to have sent the ships at all.

## Keywords
expansion underway, long view, foresight, ships at sea, established progress, patience, delay (reversed)

## Sources

- Joan Bunning, *Learning the Tarot* (Weiser, 1998).
