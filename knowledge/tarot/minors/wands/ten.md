# Ten of Wands

## Image
A figure trudges toward a distant village, bent under a bundle of ten heavy wands he carries with both arms. The wands obscure his face. He is on foot, on a dirt road.

## Core Meaning
The over-burdened completion. The work has been won — the wands are the questioner's — but he is now staggering under their weight. Read as responsibilities accumulated past sustainability, the success that has become its own burden.

## Upright
Tradition reads the card as the cycle's hard ending: the fire that started with the Ace has spread, and the questioner is now the one who carries all of it. There is little glory in this card; the village is in sight, but the path is hard. The card asks whether the figure is carrying what he is carrying because he must or because he has not learned to put any of it down.

## Reversed
The bundle put down, gracefully or abruptly. Sometimes the card reversed indicates the relief of finally delegating, finally refusing to carry what was never the questioner's to carry. Sometimes the bundle has crushed the figure.

## Keywords
overburden, the success-as-weight, responsibilities accumulated, near-completion, the long road, delegation (reversed), collapse (reversed)
