# Trine (△)

## Header
**Glyph:** △. **Angular distance:** 120°. **Default orb:** 8°. **Class:** soft (harmonious).

## Core Meaning
The flow between like and like. The trine places two bodies in signs of the same element — Aries-Leo-Sagittarius for fire, Taurus-Virgo-Capricorn for earth, and so on. Read as the easy aspect in which the two principles speak the same language and support each other without friction.

## Upright
Tradition reads the trine as the most fortunate of the aspects: what is connected by trine moves smoothly. A Venus-Jupiter trine gives a natural gift for love and generosity; a Mars-Saturn trine gives discipline that does not feel like a burden; a Moon-Mercury trine gives feeling and thought that translate to each other easily. Modern practice notes a caution — trines describe what comes easily, and what comes easily may not be worked or developed.

## Variants
The grand trine (three planets each 120° apart, forming a triangle in one element) is the classic configuration of natural talent in a particular domain. Tradition warns that grand-trine charts can be lazy with their gift; the easy flow may not feel like enough to push the figure to develop. The most reliable grand trines are those that include a square or opposition somewhere — friction that forces the talent to be exercised.

## In a Chart
The houses involved in a trine show where flow happens. The signs show the element being supported: a fire grand trine gives easy enthusiasm; an earth grand trine gives easy practical competence; an air grand trine gives easy thinking; a water grand trine gives easy feeling. Trines are the chart's resources — what the figure can rely on when other functions are stressed.

## Keywords
the flow, the easy aspect, the element shared, the gift, the grand trine, the resource, the natural talent, the caution against laziness
