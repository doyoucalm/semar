# Page of Pentacles

## Image
A young figure stands in a green field, holding a pentacle aloft on both hands, studying it intently. Mountains in the distance; ploughed fields visible.

## Core Meaning
The student of substance. The Page of Pentacles is the apprentice whose curiosity is for how things actually work — money, body, craft, the soil itself. Read as the patient learner, the careful starter, the figure who is willing to do the unglamorous early work.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the student attentive to the syllabus, the young craftsperson in apprenticeship, the figure who has just been offered a real opportunity and is taking it seriously. The pentacle is being looked at as if it were a riddle — the Page's gift is to give the material world the attention it deserves.

## Reversed
The study abandoned, or the apprenticeship taken on for the wrong reasons. Sometimes the Page reversed is the figure whose ambition has skipped past the patient years required.

## Keywords
the patient learner, careful starter, apprenticeship, the studied pentacle, attention to substance, abandoned study (reversed)
