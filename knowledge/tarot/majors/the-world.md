# The World (XXI)

## Image
A naked figure — sometimes androgynous, sometimes plainly a woman — dances inside an oval wreath of laurel. She holds two short wands, one in each hand. The four corners of the card show the four living creatures: a winged angel, an eagle, a lion, a bull. The wreath is bound at top and bottom by red ribbons.

## Core Meaning
The completion that is also a beginning. The World is the final card of the Major Arcana, the last station on the journey that began with the Fool. Read as wholeness, integration, the achievement of the cycle, and — crucially — the recognition that the cycle's end is the threshold of the next one. The figure dances; she does not rest. The wreath is open at top and bottom; one can pass through.

## Upright
The four creatures in the corners are the same ones that frame the Wheel of Fortune — the fixed signs, the four elements, the four evangelists in the Christian reading. Where they framed the Wheel of fate, here they frame the dancer of fulfilment: the same forces that turn the world also bear witness to the figure who has integrated them. The two wands in her hands echo the Magician's single wand — what was set out as potential at the start is now held doubled, in conscious mastery. Tradition reads the upright card as the long project completed, the journey home, the recognition of one's own life as a unified shape, and the readiness for what comes next.

## Reversed
Completion deferred or refused. Reversed, the World may indicate the long project nearly finished but not quite — the last 10% that is somehow harder than the first 90%. On the other side, the same reversal points to the failure to receive completion — the questioner unable to stop and recognise what has been finished, already rushing into the next thing without integrating the last.

## In a Reading
Beside Judgement, the pair completes the journey — the awakening followed by the integration. Beside the Fool, the start and the end of the cycle, with the recognition that they meet. In a project reading, the World is the literal completion sign; in a life-stage reading, the chapter recognised as whole. The card is the deck's most affirming outcome.

## Keywords
completion, wholeness, integration, the dance, the journey done, threshold to the next cycle, fulfilment, near-completion (reversed), unintegrated finish (reversed)
