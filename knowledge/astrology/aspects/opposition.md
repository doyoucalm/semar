# Opposition (☍)

## Header
**Glyph:** ☍. **Angular distance:** 180°. **Default orb:** 8°. **Class:** hard (challenging).

## Core Meaning
The encounter across the chart. The opposition places two bodies in opposite signs, opposite houses, opposite zodiac points. Read as the dynamic of polarity — two principles that demand each other's attention and that the figure must learn to hold in balance rather than collapse to one side.

## Upright
Tradition reads the opposition as a "hard" aspect — it generates tension and conflict — but modern practice treats it as the most relational of the hard aspects. The opposition wants to be seen by another: oppositions in the chart often play out through other people, partners, opponents, mirrors. The signs involved are always complementary (Aries-Libra, Taurus-Scorpio) and share an axis of meaning even as they pull in different directions.

## Variants
The opposition is the central aspect of the seventh house and of the lunar full phase. Full moons are oppositions of Sun and Moon; the felt quality of full moons — exposure, completion, the public arrival of what was begun — applies to oppositions generally. A Sun-Moon opposition in the natal chart often gives a figure born to negotiate between two parts of the self that resist integration.

## In a Chart
The houses involved in an opposition show where the polarity plays out. The planets show what is opposed: Mars-Venus oppositions stage the tension between desire and value; Saturn-Jupiter oppositions stage the tension between contraction and expansion. The work of an opposition is rarely "resolve to one side" — it is "learn to live the swing."

## Keywords
the polarity, the encounter, the seventh-house dynamic, the full moon, the swing, the mirror, the opponent, the negotiated balance
