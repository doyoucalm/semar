# Justice (XI)

## Image
A crowned figure sits on a stone throne between two pillars, holding an upright sword in one hand and balanced scales in the other. The figure faces forward, gaze level. A purple curtain hangs behind. The sword's blade is double-edged; the scales are perfectly even.

## Core Meaning
Cause and effect made visible. Justice is the card that weighs what has been done and returns the measure. Read as fairness, accountability, legal matters in the literal sense, and the more general principle that actions accumulate weight and at some point are weighed. The sword is the discriminating intellect; the scales are the equilibrium it serves.

## Upright
This figure is not personalised — Justice does not wear the blindfold of later allegory, but her gaze is impersonal in its own way: she sees clearly without favour. The double-edged sword is significant; it cuts in both directions. Tradition reads the upright card as the legal verdict that goes the questioner's way only if their case is in fact just, as the relationship rebalancing after long imbalance, and as the more contemplative work of self-honesty: weighing one's own actions against one's own values, not against another's. The card is associated with the sign Libra in the Golden Dawn system.

## Reversed
The scales tipped. Reversed, Justice may indicate the verdict that fell wrong, the unfair outcome, or — internally — the bias that has been allowed to operate unexamined. The other shadow is the refusal of accountability: when one's actions have weight and one declines to feel it. The sword still cuts, but it cuts crookedly.

## In a Reading
Beside the Wheel, the personal-versus-impersonal question of agency. Beside the Lovers, choice weighed for ethical clarity. In a legal reading, Justice is a literal indicator: the verdict will reflect the truth of the case. In an inner reading, the card calls for the unflattering review of one's own ledger.

## Keywords
fairness, accountability, balance, the sword, the scales, legal matters, cause and effect, Libra, bias (reversed), evasion of consequence (reversed)
