# Page of Swords

## Image
A young figure stands on a windy hilltop, holding a sword aloft and looking out to the side. Birds gather in the sky behind him; clouds streak past. His hair and his cloak are blown by wind.

## Core Meaning
The watchful young one. The Page of Swords is the figure whose mind is on the move — curious, alert, sometimes prone to verbal sharpness before wisdom catches up. Read as the messenger of information, the student of ideas, the difficult truth carried by a young carrier.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the apprentice of the mind: the student, the investigator, the young person whose questions are good and whose patience is short. News of an intellectual or contractual nature; sometimes a vigilant friend who carries the difficult report.

## Reversed
Sharpness without insight. The Page reversed may be the gossip, the careless tongue, the figure who weaponises information they have not yet understood.

## Keywords
watchful young one, the investigator, message of mind, alert, intellectual apprentice, sharp tongue (reversed), gossip (reversed)
