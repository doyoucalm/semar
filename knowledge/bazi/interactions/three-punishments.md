# Three Punishments (三刑)

## Header
**Type:** branch punishment triad or pair. **Configurations:**
- **No favour (无恩之刑):** 寅 → 巳 → 申 → 寅 (loop)
- **Ungrateful (恃势之刑):** 丑 → 戌 → 未 → 丑 (loop)
- **Wuli / impolite (无礼之刑):** 子 ↔ 卯
- **Self-punishment (自刑):** 辰辰 / 午午 / 酉酉 / 亥亥

## Core Meaning
The branches that wound each other. Where the clash is a clean strike, the punishment is a more tangled wound — a hidden grudge, an undermining, a problem that returns. Read as the chart's recurrent troubles: legal trouble, betrayal, family conflict, slow self-damage.

## No Favour (寅巳申)
The "favour-less" punishment, often appearing in charts involved with legal matters or chronic conflicts in officialdom. Tradition reads it as the three parties who should have helped each other and instead undermine each other. When all three branches appear in the chart, the punishment is at full strength; when two appear, partial punishment.

## Ungrateful (丑戌未)
The "force-and-relying" punishment. The three earth branches that punish each other through over-reliance on position. Tradition reads it as friends, family, or institutions who should have supported the figure but instead drain. Charts with this triad often have inheritance disputes, business-partner trouble, or family quarrels that span generations.

## Impolite (子卯)
The "no-courtesy" punishment between water and wood. The 子-卯 pair is sometimes read as marital trouble, especially when 子 or 卯 sit on the day branch. Tradition reads it as the figure who expects too much from a partner or whose partner expects too much from them — punishment by impoliteness, by withheld respect.

## Self-Punishment (自刑)
When a branch appears twice in the same chart (年柱 and 时柱 both being 辰, for example), the branch punishes itself. Tradition reads this as internal conflict, self-sabotage, the figure who keeps undoing their own work. The four self-punishing branches are 辰, 午, 酉, 亥.

## In a Chart
Punishments are subtler than clashes. They show up less as one dramatic event and more as recurrent pattern: the legal trouble that keeps returning, the friend who keeps disappointing, the project that keeps going wrong in the same way. The remedy is rarely to remove the punishment — it is woven into the chart — but to understand it, name it, and stop expecting it to resolve.

## Keywords
the wound, the recurrent trouble, betrayal, legal trouble, family conflict, self-sabotage, the hidden grudge
