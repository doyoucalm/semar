# Eight of Cups

## Image
A cloaked figure walks away into mountains under a partly-eclipsed moon. Eight cups are stacked behind him — a tower with a missing cup at top. He carries a staff.

## Core Meaning
The conscious leaving. The figure has built something — the stack of cups — and is choosing to walk away from it because the missing cup at the top signals the structure cannot be completed where it stands. Read as the principled departure, the emotional pilgrimage, the leaving that is not abandonment but search.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the resignation from the position that no longer matches the soul, the leaving of a relationship that is intact but not enough, the quiet pilgrimage. The moon is the High Priestess's element; this departure is guided by inner knowing.

## Reversed
Leaving refused, or leaving impulsive. The figure stays where the stack is broken because departure feels too hard; or leaves before the leaving is ripe, taking the unfinished situation with him.

## Keywords
the conscious leaving, principled departure, pilgrimage, the missing top-cup, soul-search, staying when one should go (reversed), running (reversed)
