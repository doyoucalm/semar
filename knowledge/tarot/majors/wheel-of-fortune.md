# Wheel of Fortune (X)

## Image
A great wheel turns in the sky, marked with the Hebrew letters of YHVH and the alchemical glyphs of mercury, sulphur, water, and salt. A jackal-headed figure climbs one side; a snake descends the other; a winged sphinx sits crowned at the top, holding a sword. In the four corners, four winged beings read books — angel, eagle, lion, bull.

## Core Meaning
The turn of fate. The Wheel is the moment the situation moves of its own accord, by forces beyond personal will. Read as luck, cycle, the rise and fall, and the long perspective that recognises both fortune and misfortune as positions on the same wheel. The card is not a verdict; it is a reminder that the wheel does not stop.

## Upright
The figure at the top — the sphinx — does not climb; she is at rest. The card teaches that the still point is not on the rim but at the centre, and that the rim's movements are not personal. The four corner-beings (lion, bull, eagle, angel — the fixed signs of the zodiac and the four evangelists in another reading) frame the wheel with the steady text. Tradition reads the upright card as a turn of luck, an unforeseen opening, a season-change. The figure being lifted on the wheel is not virtuous, only positioned; the figure descending is not in disgrace, only on the other arc.

## Reversed
The wheel jammed, or the cycle resisted. Reversed, the card may mark a turn for the worse, a streak of misfortune. More often, it points to the questioner's refusal to acknowledge the cycle — clinging to the high position as if it were the only one, or to the low one as if it would never end. The shadow is the wheel taken personally.

## In a Reading
Beside Justice, the Wheel asks how much of the situation is personal action and how much is impersonal turn. Beside Death, both are non-negotiable; one is direction-changing, the other is cyclical. In a question about timing, the Wheel almost always says wait for the turn. In a question about agency, it counsels accepting what is not in one's hands.

## Keywords
cycle, fortune, fate, the turn, luck, change, perspective, the still centre, misfortune (reversed), refusing the cycle (reversed)
