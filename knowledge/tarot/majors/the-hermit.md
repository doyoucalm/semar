# The Hermit (IX)

## Image
A grey-robed figure stands on a snowy peak, alone. He holds aloft a lantern containing a six-pointed star. His other hand rests on a wooden staff. The light from the lantern is small, but it reaches forward into the dark.

## Core Meaning
Withdrawal in service of seeing. The Hermit is the figure who has stepped away from the road in order to see the road clearly. Read as solitude, contemplation, the inward turn — and the steady, deliberate carrying of a small light through dark country. The lantern is not the sun; the Hermit does not promise illumination of everything. He promises a single step's worth.

## Upright
The Hermit's grey is not gloom; it is the colour of accumulated experience that has gone beyond the bright colours of the early cards. He is the master who has gone up the mountain to think, not the recluse who has gone there to hide. The lantern's six-pointed star — a Solomon's seal — links the figure to the wisdom tradition: what he carries is not new, but it must be carried personally, by him, into the dark. In a reading the card points to the retreat one needs in order to see, to the teacher whose method is to walk alongside rather than instruct, and to the quality of solitude that is companionship rather than loneliness.

## Reversed
Withdrawal as evasion. Reversed, the Hermit may indicate isolation that has gone past usefulness — the cave that has become the world. On the other side, the same reversal points to the refusal to retreat when retreat is needed, to the over-busied life that has forgotten how to be alone. The lantern in either case has gone dim.

## In a Reading
Beside the High Priestess, the Hermit's introspection meets the Priestess's intuition — two modes of inner knowing, both quiet. Beside the Hierophant, solitary wisdom versus institutional wisdom. In a career reading he counsels reflection before the next move; in a relationship reading he asks whether the questioner needs more solitude than the situation allows, or has retreated too far from contact.

## Keywords
solitude, contemplation, inward turn, the lantern, the teacher, wisdom, retreat, withdrawal, isolation (reversed), refusing solitude (reversed)
