# Seven of Pentacles

## Image
A young farmer leans on a hoe, looking at a vine on which six pentacles grow as fruit; one pentacle rests on the ground at his feet. His expression is contemplative — neither pleased nor disappointed.

## Core Meaning
Patient cultivation. The card is the pause halfway through a long project — the moment when the figure considers what the work has yielded so far and whether the long approach should continue. Read as long-haul patience, evaluation, the slow harvest.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the pause in the year's work to take stock: not yet harvest, no longer planting, the long stretch where growth is happening invisibly. The figure's expression is honest — there is some weariness, some pride, no false enthusiasm. The card asks the questioner to honour what has been built so far without rushing the conclusion.

## Reversed
The pause turned into despair, or the patience exhausted. The figure abandons the field too soon, or stays past the harvest hoping the vine will produce more than it can.

## Keywords
patient cultivation, long-haul work, the harvest evaluation, slow growth, weariness honoured, abandonment (reversed), overstaying (reversed)
