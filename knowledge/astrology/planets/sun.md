# Sun (☉)

## Glyph
A circle with a central dot — the wholeness with its centre marked. The oldest of the planetary symbols, found in Egyptian and Mesopotamian seals.

## Header
**Domicile:** Leo. **Exaltation:** Aries (19°). **Detriment:** Aquarius. **Fall:** Libra. **Sect:** diurnal. **Mythic figure:** Apollo; Helios; Ra; Surya.

## Core Meaning
The centre of the chart. The Sun is identity, vitality, the part of the figure that says "I." Read as the core selfhood — what the chart is for, what it organises itself around, what burns steadily at the middle.

## Upright
Tradition reads the Sun as the king, the father, the heart, the source of light. The sign holding the Sun describes the costume of the self; the house holding the Sun describes the room where that self most fully appears; the aspects to the Sun describe what helps or interferes with its shining. The dignified Sun is the figure at home in their own life — generous, warm, recognisable, capable of acting on what they actually want.

## Afflicted
The same centre dimmed. A Sun in detriment, fall, or hard aspect to malefics may indicate a figure who has not yet stepped into their own life — the identity borrowed from a parent, the self that defers to please, the heart that cannot find its own warmth. Tradition reads combust planets (within 8° of the Sun) as overwhelmed by the king; the planet's themes are eclipsed.

## In a Chart
The Sun's sign is the first answer to "who is this person?" — but only the first. The Ascendant gives the body, the Moon gives the feeling, the Sun gives the purpose. The Sun's house shows where the figure most wants to be seen and where the work of self-expression happens. Solar returns (the chart cast for the Sun's exact return each year) are read as the year's headline.

## Keywords
identity, vitality, the centre, the king, the father, the heart, purpose, ego (shadow when isolated)
