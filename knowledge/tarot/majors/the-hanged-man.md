# The Hanged Man (XII)

## Image
A young man hangs by one foot from a T-shaped wooden gallows, suspended upside-down. His other leg is crossed behind the first, forming a numeral 4 inverted. His hands are bound behind his back. His expression is serene. A halo of light surrounds his head. The tree he hangs from is still alive — leaves grow from it.

## Core Meaning
The reversed perspective. The Hanged Man is the figure who, having stopped struggling, sees what cannot be seen from upright. Read as surrender, suspension, the deliberate giving-up of the customary view in exchange for a different angle. The card teaches that some understandings are not available through effort; they require the inversion.

## Upright
This card is the still point in the middle of the Major Arcana — the figure who pauses between the impersonal verdict of Justice and the irrevocable change of Death. He hangs willingly; the rope is loose enough that he could free himself if he chose. The halo signals that this is not punishment but initiation. Tradition links the imagery to Odin's nine days on the world-tree and to the shamanic ordeal that delivers vision. The card asks the questioner to recognise what is being seen because the customary footing has been removed.

## Reversed
The pause refused, or the pause overstayed. Reversed, the Hanged Man may indicate the stubborn refusal to suspend a position long enough to see it differently; or, on the other side, the indefinite suspension that has become avoidance. The serene face turns into the strained one of someone who is making the wait into a virtue.

## In a Reading
Beside Death, the difference between conscious suspension and the change that arrives unbidden. Beside the Hermit, two modes of inward turn — one in retreat, one in willing inversion. In a career reading he indicates the project that must be held in abeyance for an answer to emerge; in a relationship reading, the situation where one party must stop pulling on the rope.

## Keywords
surrender, suspension, reversed perspective, willing pause, sacrifice, initiation, stuck (reversed), avoidance (reversed)
