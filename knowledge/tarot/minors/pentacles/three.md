# Three of Pentacles

## Image
A stonemason works at the entrance of a stone cathedral, chisel and mallet in hand. A monk and a noble — patron and architect — stand consulting plans, looking up at him.

## Core Meaning
Craft and collaboration. The apprentice mason is doing the work; the architect's plan and the patron's money are visible behind him. Read as skilled work in its institutional setting, the team in which each person knows their role.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the apprenticeship paying off — the work being recognised, the skill respected, the project moving forward through collaboration. The cathedral is significant: this is long-form work, built across generations, requiring more than one craftsperson and more than one season.

## Reversed
Collaboration broken — the patron interfering, the architect contradicting, the apprentice's skill ignored. Sometimes the cathedral has been started without enough planning.

## Keywords
craft, collaboration, apprenticeship recognised, the team, the cathedral project, skilled work, broken team (reversed)
