# Three of Swords

## Image
Three swords pierce a red heart, set against a grey, rainy sky. No human figure appears.

## Core Meaning
Heartbreak. The card depicts what cannot be euphemised — the wound to the heart, the painful truth, the loss that the mind has not been able to defend against. Read as grief in its mental form, the bitter clarity that hurts.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the moment of being told the difficult truth, the news that lands, the betrayal whose evidence cannot be argued away. The grey sky is significant; this is weather, not a passing cloud. The card does not promise quick recovery, but it does promise that the truth, however hurtful, is now visible — and that is a beginning of something.

## Reversed
Recovery beginning, or grief denied. The pierced heart starts to heal; or the swords are withdrawn but the wound is being papered over rather than tended.

## Keywords
heartbreak, painful truth, grief, the pierced heart, betrayal acknowledged, weather of sorrow, recovery (reversed), denial (reversed)
