# Six of Wands

## Image
A rider on a white horse, laurel-wreathed, returns through a crowd. A second laurel wreath is fixed to his upraised wand. Other figures hold their wands upright in greeting.

## Core Meaning
Victory acknowledged. The contest is over, the result is in, and the questioner returns with the wreath. Read as public success, recognition, the parade home.

## Upright
This is one of the brightest cards in the suit. Tradition reads it as the project that has won the award, the candidate who has won the election, the athlete who has won the race. There is well-earned pride here; the wreaths are real. The crowd's salute matters too — the victory is recognised by community, not only by the self.

## Reversed
The victory delayed, denied, or won at a cost the figure has not yet acknowledged. Sometimes the pride of the rider has tipped into arrogance — the laurel mistaken for a permanent crown.

## Keywords
victory, recognition, public success, the laurel, the parade home, well-earned pride, arrogance (reversed), delayed recognition (reversed)
