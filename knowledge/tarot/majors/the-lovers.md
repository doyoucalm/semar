# The Lovers (VI)

## Image
A man and a woman stand beneath a winged angel. Behind the woman, the Tree of Knowledge bears five fruit and a coiled serpent; behind the man, the Tree of Life bears twelve flames. A bare mountain rises between them; the sun blazes above. The angel — usually identified as Raphael — extends a benediction over the pair.

## Core Meaning
The card of choice and of relationship. The Lovers is not only about romance; it is about every situation where one stands at the choosing-point between two paths, two loves, two ways of being. Read as union, as conscious commitment, and as the kind of decision that cannot be unmade because it shapes who one becomes.

## Upright
The angel's blessing falls equally on both figures, but its presence answers an unspoken question: there is something larger than the pair, witnessing the choice. The Tree of Knowledge — with its serpent, its echo of Eden — is on the side of the woman; the Tree of Life is on the side of the man. The card refuses the easy reading of innocence-corrupted; the choice between knowing and not-knowing is the choice that defines the human. Read as relationship, the card is the moment two separate lives become one shape — by choice, never by inheritance.

## Reversed
The choice deferred, or the union built on the wrong foundation. Reversed, the Lovers may indicate indecision, the inability to commit to either path; or a relationship grounded in obligation rather than chosen love. The mountain between the figures has grown; the angel's blessing seems absent. The shadow form also includes the romance taken as escape — the union sought to avoid choosing oneself.

## In a Reading
Beside the Devil, the Lovers asks whether the bond is chosen or compulsive; beside Justice, the choice is being weighed for fairness; beside the Hermit, the choice may be between connection and solitude. In a career or values reading, the card points to the decision that will commit one to a particular life. The romantic reading is the most common but rarely the deepest.

## Keywords
choice, union, relationship, commitment, partnership, values alignment, the angel-witness, indecision (reversed), misalignment (reversed)

## Sources
- Arthur Edward Waite, *The Pictorial Key to the Tarot* (Rider, 1910) — the Eden imagery is Waite's; in earlier Marseille decks the card is closer to a Roman *amor* scene.
- Rachel Pollack, *Seventy-Eight Degrees of Wisdom* (Aquarian Press, 1980) — the card as choice-point rather than romance card.
- Mary K. Greer, *Tarot for Yourself* (Newcastle, 1984) — practical work with the card in decision spreads.
