# General Star (将星)

## Header
**Category:** authority / command. **Anchor:** year or day branch. **Rule:** the year (or day) branch determines a single branch — always the "cardinal" branch of the triad — that, if present elsewhere in the chart, brings the General Star.

## Lookup
| Year/Day branch | General Star |
| --- | --- |
| 申, 子, 辰 | 子 |
| 亥, 卯, 未 | 卯 |
| 寅, 午, 戌 | 午 |
| 巳, 酉, 丑 | 酉 |

The General Star is always the peak branch of the elemental triad — the same position the day branch holds in the Peach Blossom system but pointing to the home branch rather than the diagonally-offset one.

## Core Meaning
The star of command. The General Star represents leadership, authority, the capacity to direct others and to be obeyed. Read as the figure who is comfortable in charge, who can hold a position of responsibility through difficulty, who is born to lead in some domain.

## In a Chart
A chart with the General Star in the month or day branch is the most strongly placed — leadership becomes a central pattern of the life. In the hour branch, the figure may come into leadership later in life or through their children. The star is most useful when supported by a strong day master and clean structure; a weak chart with the General Star may have the desire to lead without the resources to do it.

The classical formulation: "All who reach high office have the General Star." Modern practice softens this — the star indicates capacity for command but not its guaranteed expression.

## When Activated
The star is activated by luck pillars or annual transits bringing its branch into play. A General Star year often coincides with promotions, leadership opportunities, or sudden authority — the figure asked to step into command, often unexpectedly.

## Keywords
the commander, authority, leadership, the position of responsibility, command capacity, the directing function
