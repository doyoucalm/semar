# King of Swords

## Image
A crowned king sits on a stone throne, sword held upright over his right shoulder. He wears a blue robe under his armour; butterflies and angels are carved into the throne. The sky behind is blue with thin cloud.

## Core Meaning
Mind mastered with authority. The King of Swords is the judge, the strategist, the leader whose decisions are based on principle rather than feeling. Read as intellectual sovereignty, ethical clarity, the figure to whom one brings the hard question.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the judge in the court, the senior counsellor, the strategist whose advice is precise. The sword is held over the shoulder, not pointed; he does not need to wave it. The blue robe under armour is significant — his clarity is grounded in feeling, not divorced from it.

## Reversed
Authority gone arbitrary, or principle weaponised. The King reversed may be the figure who uses law to oppress, the strategist whose intellect has become manipulation, the patriarch whose decisions are coded as principle but driven by something else.

## Keywords
intellectual sovereignty, the judge, the strategist, ethical clarity, principled command, manipulation (reversed), arbitrary authority (reversed)
