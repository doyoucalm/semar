# The Tower (XVI)

## Image
A tall grey tower built on a mountaintop is struck by a bolt of lightning from a black sky. A golden crown is blown off the top by the strike. Two figures fall from the tower, headfirst, their robes whipping. Twenty-two flames — yods, in the Hebrew letter — fall around them. Below, fire and smoke; no ground visible.

## Core Meaning
The structure that fell. The Tower is the card of the sudden collapse — the disillusionment that takes the edifice down, the revelation that cannot be put back, the disaster that arrives without warning. Read as upheaval, as the falling-away of false foundation, and (in its harder hour) as catastrophe. The crown blown loose is the central image: the prize is the first thing to go.

## Upright
The lightning is not punishment, though it can feel like it. The Tower was built on something that would not hold, and the strike is the moment the structural fault is revealed by the sky. Tradition reads the upright card as the sudden revelation, the broken relationship, the lost position, the worldview that no longer works. There is grief in the card; there is also clarity. The flame-yods scattered through the air are the seeds of a different building — once the dust settles. The card is associated with Mars in the Golden Dawn system.

## Reversed
The collapse averted, or delayed, or in slow motion. Reversed, the Tower may indicate the structure that ought to fall and has not yet — the relationship limping along on broken foundation, the project everyone knows is doomed but nobody will name. On the lighter side, the reversed card sometimes marks the warning given before the actual fall, the early sign that change is coming.

## In a Reading
Beside Death, two registers of ending — Death the slow turn, the Tower the sudden break. Beside the Star, the bright hope that follows after the catastrophe is processed. In a relationship reading the Tower often points to the revelation that ends the form; in a career reading, the firing or the public failure. The card is hard to read gently, and gentle reading often serves the questioner less than truth does.

## Keywords
sudden collapse, revelation, upheaval, false foundation, the lightning, disillusionment, Mars, breaking, averted collapse (reversed), warning (reversed)

## Sources

- Rachel Pollack, *Seventy-Eight Degrees of Wisdom* (Aquarian Press, 1980) — the Tower as the necessary breakdown that precedes the Star.
- Mary K. Greer, *Tarot for Yourself* (Newcastle, 1984) — handling Tower readings with care.
