# Moon (☽)

## Glyph
A crescent. The shape of the waxing or waning sliver — the body that changes.

## Header
**Domicile:** Cancer. **Exaltation:** Taurus (3°). **Detriment:** Capricorn. **Fall:** Scorpio. **Sect:** nocturnal. **Mythic figure:** Selene; Artemis; Diana; Chang'e; the mother goddesses.

## Core Meaning
The feeling body. The Moon is mood, memory, instinct, the part of the figure that responds before thinking. Read as the inner private life — what the figure needs to feel safe, what they reach for in the dark, what conditions allow rest.

## Upright
Tradition reads the Moon as the mother, the body, the night, the rhythm of tides. The sign holding the Moon describes the temperament — what flavour the feeling has. The house holding the Moon describes where the figure is emotionally most at home and where mood most colours behaviour. A dignified Moon makes the inner life steady, the body well-fed, the emotional weather workable.

## Afflicted
The same body unsettled. A Moon in detriment, fall, or hard aspect to malefics may indicate a figure who has not received what they needed early — the mother absent or overwhelming, the body never quite trusted, the emotional weather wild. Tradition watches the Moon's phase too: a Moon close to the Sun is hidden; a full Moon is exposed; a Moon waning into darkness is closer to the unconscious.

## In a Chart
The Moon's sign is the answer to "what does this person need to feel held?" The Moon's house is where that need looks for satisfaction. The Moon's aspects to other planets show what helps or interferes with the feeling body — Moon-Saturn often gives early restriction; Moon-Neptune often gives porousness; Moon-Venus often gives a love of beauty as comfort. The progressed Moon (about 28 years per zodiac) is read as the long unfolding of emotional life.

## Keywords
mood, the body, the mother, the night, instinct, memory, comfort, neediness (shadow), reactivity (shadow)
