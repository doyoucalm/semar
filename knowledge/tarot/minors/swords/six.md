# Six of Swords

## Image
A ferryman poles a small boat across calm water toward a distant shore. A hooded figure and a child sit in the boat. Six swords are stuck upright in the bow. The water on the far side of the boat is smooth; on the near side, it is choppy.

## Core Meaning
The passage to calmer water. The figure is leaving a difficult chapter, carrying what was useful (the swords), being ferried — by someone, or by circumstance — toward a more peaceful shore. Read as transition, the journey out of trouble, the quiet exodus.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the move away from a hard situation — leaving a job, a relationship, a country, a chapter — without drama, with the sorrow of what is being left honoured. The card is one of the most consoling in the suit; it promises that the rough water is not permanent.

## Reversed
The passage delayed, or the figure unable to leave the rough water. Sometimes the move has been made but the figure has brought the trouble with her; sometimes she is still on the difficult shore wishing she had moved sooner.

## Keywords
passage, transition, leaving rough water, the ferry, quiet exodus, the difficult move, stuck (reversed), unfinished leaving (reversed)
