# Queen of Pentacles

## Image
A crowned woman sits on a throne carved with goats, cherubs, and fruits, in a garden. She holds a large pentacle in her lap, looking down at it tenderly. A rabbit hops at her feet; roses and pears grow around her.

## Core Meaning
Earth mastered with abundance. The Queen of Pentacles is the figure who keeps both the books and the garden with the same care — the generous host of the material life, the matriarch whose hospitality is both warm and well-managed. Read as practical wisdom, the nourishing presence, the steady abundance.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the woman who has built a sustainable life: enough money, a good garden, a warm home, animals around her. She is sensual without being decadent, generous without being depleted. Her wealth is not for display; it is for use. The card praises the practical matron, the small-business owner, the friend whose home is a refuge.

## Reversed
Abundance turned controlling, or hospitality turned obligation. The Queen reversed may indicate the figure whose care has become possessive, or whose financial stability has become anxiety about loss. Sometimes the garden has become the cage.

## Keywords
practical wisdom, nourishing presence, steady abundance, the garden matriarch, sensual without decadence, controlling care (reversed), financial anxiety (reversed)
