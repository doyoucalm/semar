# Four of Swords

## Image
A knight lies on a stone tomb in a chapel, hands folded in prayer. Three swords hang on the wall above; one sword is carved beneath him. A stained-glass window depicts a peaceful scene.

## Core Meaning
Rest after wounding. The figure has come through a difficult passage and lies still — not dead, but in the recovery that requires stillness. Read as recuperation, retreat for healing, the necessary pause after the storm.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the convalescence, the meditation retreat, the sleep that follows a long stressful chapter. The chapel is the protected space; the figure has earned the rest by surviving the previous cards. Sometimes the card warns that rest is not optional — the body will take it whether the schedule allows or not.

## Reversed
The rest refused, or the rest overstayed. Sometimes the figure rises too soon; sometimes the chapel has become an avoidance of return to the world.

## Keywords
rest, recuperation, retreat, sanctuary, the necessary pause, meditation, refused rest (reversed), avoidance (reversed)
