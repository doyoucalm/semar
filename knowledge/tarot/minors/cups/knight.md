# Knight of Cups

## Image
An armoured knight on a white horse advances at a walk. He holds a cup forward, ceremonially. His helmet is winged; fish are embroidered on his cloak. A river runs through the landscape.

## Core Meaning
The romantic envoy. The Knight of Cups is the figure of courtly love, the offering of beauty, the proposal carried with care. Read as the seeker after love, the artist with a gift to present, the offer made gracefully.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the suitor, the friend who arrives with a kindness, the proposal that has emotional weight. The horse walks rather than charges; this is feeling brought forward without haste. The knight is also the figure who values aesthetic beauty — the artist's quest.

## Reversed
Romance that does not deliver — the figure whose offers are theatrical rather than real, the seducer, the dreamer who promises more than he carries. Sometimes the offering itself is sincere but rejected.

## Keywords
romantic envoy, the suitor, courtly offering, aesthetic quest, the proposal, theatricality (reversed), seduction (reversed)
