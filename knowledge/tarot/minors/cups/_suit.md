# Suit of Cups — Water

## Element & Sphere
**Water.** The suit of feeling, relationship, dream, and the inward life of imagination. Where Wands burn forward into action and Swords cut through to ideas, Cups hold what is felt — love, sorrow, longing, intuition, art, devotion. In life-domain terms: emotional life, intimacy, the unconscious, the creative dream, and the body of inherited family feeling one carries whether one knows it or not.

## Astrological Association
The water signs — Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces. Cancer especially carries the suit's home and lineage themes; Scorpio its depth and grief; Pisces its mystical and dissolving qualities.

## Visual Signature
Each cup in the Rider-Waite-Smith deck is depicted full or being filled — water visible, sometimes overflowing, sometimes spilling. The suit's mood is contemplative; figures often look downward or inward rather than out at the viewer.

## Pip Progression
The Ace is the cup of grace, offered from a hand in the clouds, water overflowing. The two through ten trace love and feeling through its stations — connection (2), celebration (3), apathy or stagnation (4), grief over what is lost while what remains is overlooked (5), nostalgic return (6), choice among many tempting futures (7), the conscious leaving of an emotional situation (8), wish-fulfilment (9), and the family or domestic completion (10).

## Court Archetypes
- **Page of Cups** — the dreamer, the artistic young one, the message that arrives from intuition or from the heart.
- **Knight of Cups** — the romantic envoy, the seeker after beauty and connection, sometimes prone to glamour.
- **Queen of Cups** — emotional mastery with feeling kept whole; the empath who has learned to hold without dissolving.
- **King of Cups** — feeling mastered without being suppressed; the leader who is moved by what he sees but is not swept by it.

## Reading Note
Cups answer questions of relationship, emotional truth, family pattern, and inner life. In health they ask about the heart and the emotional body; in work, about whether the work nourishes the soul; in relationship, about depth, honesty, and the river that flows or does not flow between two people.
