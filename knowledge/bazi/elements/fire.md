# Fire (火)

## Header
**Yang form:** 丙 bǐng (the sun, the great flame). **Yin form:** 丁 dīng (the candle, the lantern, the hearth fire). **Season:** summer. **Direction:** south. **Body:** heart and small intestine, blood vessels, tongue. **Generates:** earth. **Controls:** metal. **Controlled by:** water. **Drained by:** earth. **Resented by:** water (when fire is weak water extinguishes; when fire is strong water teaches it limit).

## Core Meaning
The element of radiance, of full visible expression. Fire is the height of summer, the heat that lets the harvest mature. Read as the principle of light, of recognition, of the warmth that gathers others to itself.

## Yang Fire (丙)
Tradition reads 丙 as the sun itself — bright, generous, impossible to hide. A 丙 day master is often warm, charismatic, and visible in any room. The figure brings energy to a gathering and tires when there is no one to shine on. 丙 fire that is well-supported is the natural leader; without support, it burns out quickly.

## Yin Fire (丁)
Tradition reads 丁 as the candle and the hearth — fire that warms a single room rather than the whole sky. A 丁 day master is often subtle, attentive, drawn to detail and to private work. The figure tends to be the steady warmth a friend or family relies on. 丁 fire is craftier than 丙 — the candle that does not blow out in the wind has learned to use its container.

## When Strong
Strong fire (born in summer, surrounded by wood and fire) wants something to illuminate. The strong fire person is the performer, the orator, the one with the visible gift. Strong fire needs metal (to forge into something useful) or water (to balance and prevent burnout). Strong fire without water becomes the figure whose brightness consumes itself.

## When Weak
Weak fire (born in winter, surrounded by water, or facing strong earth that drains it) needs wood to feed it and warmth to sustain it. The weak fire person may feel persistently cold — emotionally, physically, in confidence. The remedy is supply: friends, mentors, places where the figure is appreciated.

## Keywords
summer, radiance, light, warmth, the sun (yang), the candle (yin), the gathering, the performance, charisma, burnout (shadow)
