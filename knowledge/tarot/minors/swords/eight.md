# Eight of Swords

## Image
A blindfolded, bound woman stands surrounded by eight swords planted in the muddy ground. The swords form a fence around her — but the fence has gaps. A castle stands on a low hill far behind.

## Core Meaning
Self-imposed limitation. The figure is bound — but the binding is light, the fence is gapped, the blindfold can be removed. Read as the trap one has talked oneself into, the helplessness that has more options than it feels.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the situation that feels inescapable but is not — the job one feels trapped in, the relationship one believes one cannot leave, the story one tells oneself about why no move is possible. The card does not deny the binding; it points out the gaps.

## Reversed
The blindfold lifted, the cords cut. The figure starts to see the gaps and walks through them. Or the binding deepens — the story tightens, the helplessness becomes habit.

## Keywords
self-imposed limitation, the bound figure, the gapped fence, the story of trap, perceived helplessness, freedom seen (reversed), tighter trap (reversed)
