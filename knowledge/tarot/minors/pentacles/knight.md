# Knight of Pentacles

## Image
An armoured knight sits on a black horse, both still. He holds a pentacle out in front of him as if examining it. Ploughed fields stretch behind. The horse is plumed but not pawing.

## Core Meaning
The steady worker. The Knight of Pentacles is the figure who rides slowly, arrives where he said he would, finishes what he started — the most reliable of the knights. Read as patience, dependability, the long methodical campaign.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the project manager whose plans get executed, the friend whose help arrives on time, the partner whose commitment is unspectacular but unshakable. The black horse is at rest; the speed of this knight is steady rather than fast. The pentacle is being inspected — he plans before he moves.

## Reversed
Steadiness gone stuck — the figure who is so cautious he never moves, the project that has been planned into the ground. On the other side, the unreliable Knight who has dropped the work in favour of distraction.

## Keywords
the steady worker, dependable, slow and certain, the long campaign, patient knight, stuck in caution (reversed), unreliability (reversed)
