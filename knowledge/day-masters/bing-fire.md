# 丙火 Bing Fire — The Radiant Sun

## Classical Source (滴天髓 Di Tian Sui)

> 丙火猛烈，欺霜侮雪。能煅庚金，逢辛反怯。土众成慈，水猖显节。虎马犬乡，甲来焚灭。

**Translation:** Bing Fire is intense; it scorns frost and insults snow. It can forge Geng Metal, but meeting Xin it turns timid. In an abundance of Earth, it grows compassionate; in a flood of Water, it reveals its integrity. In the land of Tiger (Yin), Horse (Wu), and Dog (Xu), if Jia Wood arrives, it is incinerated.

## Classical Source (子平真诠 Zi Ping Zhen Quan)

> 丙为太阳之火，阳中之阳。

**Translation:** Bing is the fire of the sun — it is the Yang within Yang.

## Modern Interpretation

**Archetype:** The Sun, the torch, the spotlight, the explosion. Bing Fire is the source of all warmth and visibility. It does not ask for permission to shine; it simply exists, and everything else orbits around it.

**Core Nature:**
丙火之人 (Bing Fire people) are generous, passionate, and fundamentally honest. They are the most extroverted of the Day Masters — they feel a biological drive to be seen and to influence. They are the "radiators" of society, giving off energy, optimism, and clarity. They despise secrecy and dark corners.

**Psychological Profile:**
Bing Fire is motivated by impact. They need to know they are making a difference, that they are enlightening the room. They have a natural "broadcasting" quality — their feelings are written on their face and their voice is often the one that sets the tone for a group.

They possess an innate sense of nobility and "rightness." Like the sun, they are constant and reliable in their output, but they can be blinding if you stand too close. They are the champions of justice and the first to call out "unfairness."

**Shadow Side:**
Impetuousness. Their intensity can burn those around them. They can be incredibly self-centered — not out of malice, but because they are the center of their own universe (the solar system model). When they lose their light, they sink into a deep, dramatic darkness.

They can also be "too honest," hurting people with blunt truths that lack nuance. "Incineration" happens when they have too much fuel (Wood) and no output (Earth/Metal/Water) — they become manic or overwhelmed.

**What Bing Fire Needs:**
- Water (壬 Ren) — "Shui Chang Xian Jie" (水猖顯節). Paradoxically, Bing needs the vastness of the ocean (Ren) to reflect its light and provide contrast. It gives the sun a "coastline" of character.
- Metal (庚 Geng) — Something to forge and transform. Bing needs a goal, a project, a "raw ore" to shape.
- Earth (戊 Wu) — To moderate the intensity (Earth grows compassionate). Earth absorbs the excess heat and makes it useful for growth.
- To avoid "Xin Metal" (辛) traps — Bing's soft spot. They can be distracted and "weakened" by shiny, pretty, or seductive things (Xin).

**Seasonal Dynamics:**
- 春 Spring (Yin/Mao month): Growing in power. Needs careful fuel (Wood) to avoid flare-ups.
- 夏 Summer (Si/Wu month): Peak intensity. Needs Water (Ren) to stay balanced and avoid scorched earth.
- 秋 Autumn (Shen/You month): Fading light. Focuses on harvest and result (Metal).
- 冬 Winter (Hai/Zi month): At its most precious. A little bit of Bing in winter provides immense hope.

**In Relationship:**
Bing Fire loves with a blazing heart. They are devoted and protective, but they need a partner who can handle the heat. They don't do well with "cold" or passive-aggressive people. They need a partner who can reflect their light or provide a stable ground (Earth) for them to land on.

**In Career:**
They excel in leadership, performance, public speaking, sales, media, and "clarifying" roles like consulting or teaching. Any role where their charisma can be a tool for collective transformation.

**The Bing Fire Question:**
When reading for a Bing Fire person, the deep question is: "What are you illuminating? And are you shining for others, or just burning for yourself?"
