# Queen of Cups

## Image
A crowned woman sits on a throne at the water's edge. She holds an ornate, closed cup — uniquely in the suit, the lid is on. She gazes at it intently. Mermaid figures are carved into the throne.

## Core Meaning
Emotional mastery with feeling kept whole. The Queen of Cups is the empath who has learned to hold without dissolving, the keeper of feeling who does not pour herself out in every cup. Read as compassion grounded, intuition trusted, the mother of the inner life.

## Upright
Tradition reads it as the wise counsellor, the woman whose presence is a held container for the feelings of others, the healer whose work depends on her own boundary as much as her openness. The closed cup is the central image: she sees what is in it, but it is not for everyone.

## Reversed
The container leaks, or the lid is welded shut. The Queen reversed may indicate emotional flooding — feelings spilling beyond control — or the opposite, the woman whose feeling-life has been so defended that warmth is no longer accessible.

## Keywords
empathic mastery, the closed cup, contained feeling, intuitive depth, compassion grounded, the healer, flooding (reversed), closure (reversed)
