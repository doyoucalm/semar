# Sword Star / Robbery Star (劫煞)

## Header
**Category:** inauspicious / hardship. **Anchor:** year or day branch. **Rule:** the year (or day) branch determines a single branch that, if present elsewhere in the chart, brings the Robbery Star.

## Lookup
| Year/Day branch | Robbery Star (劫煞) |
| --- | --- |
| 申, 子, 辰 | 巳 |
| 亥, 卯, 未 | 申 |
| 寅, 午, 戌 | 亥 |
| 巳, 酉, 丑 | 寅 |

The Robbery Star is always the "long" branch one position behind the home triad — the branch that strikes before the home triad's first long position.

## Core Meaning
The star of theft and sudden loss. The Robbery Star represents events that take from the figure without warning — accidents, theft, betrayal by close people, sudden financial loss. Read as the dark side of mobility: where the Travelling Horse moves the figure, the Robbery Star moves something away from them.

## In a Chart
A chart with the Robbery Star is read more cautiously than one without — the figure may experience repeated patterns of loss in the domain where the star sits. In the year branch, early-life loss or family difficulty. In the month branch, loss through work environment. In the day branch, loss through marriage or close partnership. In the hour branch, loss through children or in old age.

Modern practice often softens the star's reading: the figure may simply be more sensitive to financial and personal-security matters, or may need to be cautious about with whom they place trust. Multiple Robbery Star positions, however, are taken seriously as a pattern of difficulty.

## Related Stars
The Robbery Star is part of a cluster of inauspicious stars including the Disaster Star (灾煞), the Heavenly Trap (天罗) and Earthly Net (地网). Traditional readers often look at all of these together to assess the chart's exposure to misfortune.

## When Activated
The star is activated when its branch appears in a luck pillar or annual transit. Such years can coincide with financial setbacks, accidents, theft, or betrayals. The activation is intensified when accompanied by clashes or punishments.

## Keywords
theft, sudden loss, betrayal, accident, the dark side of movement, financial setback, the breach of trust
