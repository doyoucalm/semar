# Canopy (华盖)

## Header
**Category:** mystical / introspective. **Anchor:** year or day branch. **Rule:** the year (or day) branch determines a single branch — always one of the four "storage" branches 辰戌丑未 — that, if present elsewhere in the chart, brings the Canopy star.

## Core Meaning
The star of the canopy and the inner room. Canopy represents the figure's tendency toward solitude, study, spiritual practice, and the arts. Read as the inward-turning function — the figure who finds depth by withdrawing from the busy world.

## In a Chart
A chart with the Canopy tends toward introspection, scholarship, religion, mysticism, or the arts. The star is sometimes called "the lonely star" — the figure may experience a kind of loneliness or apartness even within company. This is not always read as suffering; classical sources read Canopy as a sign of refined spiritual capacity.

Multiple Canopy positions can give a figure deeply suited to monastic, scholarly, artistic, or contemplative paths. Lucky's chart (1985 with the year 乙丑 — branch 丑, and day branch 辰) carries two Canopy resonances; the chart is well-suited to inward work and to building things that honour the slow accumulation of attention.

## When the Star Is Useful
Canopy is most useful when the figure has an outlet for its depth — meditation, study, contemplative profession, art. Without an outlet, Canopy can turn into withdrawal that loses contact with the practical world.

## When Activated
The star is activated when its branch appears in a luck pillar or annual transit. Activation years often coincide with retreats, intense study, or significant spiritual or artistic deepening.

## Keywords
solitude, depth, study, the inner room, the contemplative, the arts, mysticism, the lonely star, withdrawal (shadow)
